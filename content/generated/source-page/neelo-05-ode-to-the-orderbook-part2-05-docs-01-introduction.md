# Section 1: Introduction

Part I argued that order books are the correct end-state for mature markets and that Vibe exists to help markets reach that state. Part II makes the argument sharper.

The true failure in crypto is not that order books are too slow, too centralized, or too exclusive in some moral sense. Their problem is structural: they must be selective. And once a system must be selective, it inherits the full game theory of listings.

That means gatekeeping.

That means projects optimizing for optics rather than real demand.

That means exchanges, committees, stakers, or auctions deciding what looks worthy before the market has spoken with enough depth.

The sequel thesis is simple: **the age of vibes-based listings must end.**
