# Section 4: The Hyperliquid Gap

Hyperliquid is a major step forward, but the transcript identifies the remaining gap clearly.

If the ambition is to become the house of all finance, then the system cannot rely only on a top-layer listing mechanism. It needs a full assembly line from launchpad to billion-dollar perps.

HIP-3 improves the process, but it does not eliminate the core issue:

- listings are still selective
- throughput is still limited
- the long tail is still largely unserved
- gatekeeping remains, even if it is more decentralized than before

This is not an accusation against Hyperliquid. It is simply a statement about order books. They are efficient precisely because they do not attempt to bootstrap everything.

That is why they need a lower layer.
