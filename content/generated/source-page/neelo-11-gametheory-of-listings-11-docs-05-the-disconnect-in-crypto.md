# The Disconnect in Crypto

The modern crypto market contains a growing structural disconnect.

On one side, token creation has become radically permissionless. Launchpads, bonding-curve systems, and DEX environments can produce huge numbers of new assets with near-zero friction. On the other side, serious market access remains scarce. Centralized exchanges list only a tiny number of assets. High-cost venues list only what they expect to monetize safely. Derivative markets remain even more selective.

This creates a two-world system:

- millions of tokens can be born
- only a tiny minority can access deep liquidity, leverage, or institutional attention

That gap is not just operational. It changes behavior across the entire industry.

Projects begin designing themselves around what curated venues want to see. Users migrate toward memes and trenches because that is where unpriced upside still exists. Tokens jump from zero-cost creation environments into increasingly filtered and expensive market layers. By the time a large venue is willing to list an asset, much of the open discovery phase is already over.

This is one reason low-float high-FDV launches became so common. If serious listings are scarce, expensive, and highly selective, then teams are pushed to maximize perceived prestige and valuation before real market discovery has occurred. The listing system rewards the appearance of success in advance.

The result is an industry where creation is abundant but validation is bottlenecked.

And because perpetuals sit near the top of the market-structure stack, the bottleneck becomes even more severe there. Spot access may be open. Serious shorting, hedging, and two-sided price discovery are not.
