# Section 2: The Limit of Order Books

Order books are selective by necessity. They require infrastructure, active market making, operational support, and enough real-time interest to justify the market.

That selectivity creates a gate.

A venue like Hyperliquid can list far fewer markets per year than the number of assets created on launchpads and spot DEXs. Even if the gate is well-intentioned, the result is still gatekeeping. Listings must be decided based on perceived interest rather than actual interest, because the market has not yet been fully allowed to express itself.

This creates the familiar pathologies of crypto listings:

- fake metrics to signal demand
- high listing costs
- dependence on committees, stakers, or brokers
- distorted token design around listing optics
- a bias toward narratives that look listable rather than assets that are genuinely demanded

The core limitation is not moral. It is structural. Order books are optimized for the top slice of markets, not for the chaotic process by which new markets are born.
