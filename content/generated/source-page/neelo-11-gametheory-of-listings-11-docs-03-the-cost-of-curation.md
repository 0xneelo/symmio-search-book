# The Cost of Curation

Once a venue bears meaningful downside from bad listings, its incentives change.

![Zero-cost permissionless versus high-cost curated listing systems](./assets/zero-cost-vs-curated-evolution.svg)

**Figure takeaway:** permissionless venues inherit more noise, but curated venues inherit asymmetric blame. That asymmetry is what pushes them toward higher thresholds, more filtering, and more interference with open market evolution.

A centralized exchange can lose reputation if a token performs disastrously. A high-cost on-chain listing venue can face backlash if staked governance approves obviously weak assets. A protocol that requires capital deposits or committee review creates a user expectation that someone, somewhere, is screening quality on their behalf.

That expectation matters.

The moment users believe there is curation, they also believe there is protection. And the moment a venue is expected to protect users, it has to behave conservatively. It cannot simply let everything evolve naturally. It must intervene.

That produces a chain of effects:

1. Venues raise listing thresholds.
2. Projects try to look safer, larger, and more in-demand than they really are.
3. Metrics become targets and are therefore gamed.
4. Listings shift toward assets with obvious short-term perceived demand.
5. The market loses exposure to the weird, early, uncertain assets that could have become the biggest winners.

This is why cost matters so much. Even if a system is technically decentralized, a sufficiently high listing cost can recreate the behavior of a permissioned committee. Fewer actors may hold the gatekeeping power, but gatekeeping still exists.

The issue is not simply centralization in the political sense. It is **interference with evolutionary market discovery**.

That helps explain practices that otherwise seem arbitrary: massive security deposits, selective onboarding, reputational vetoes, and extremely conservative listing menus. These are not random decisions. They are the predictable result of a venue trying to defend itself against the downside of being wrong.
