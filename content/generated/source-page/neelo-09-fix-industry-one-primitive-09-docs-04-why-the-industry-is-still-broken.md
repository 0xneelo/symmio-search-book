# Section 4: Why the Industry Is Still Broken

If anyone can launch anything, why does the industry still feel incomplete?

Because the market structure is still missing the thing that turns issuance into full price discovery.

The system is excellent at producing assets and weak at giving those assets complete market expression. Most tokens can be launched and traded on spot, but they cannot be shorted, hedged, or stress-tested in a permissionless way. This leads to familiar symptoms:

- narratives outrun fundamentals
- attention substitutes for conviction
- price discovery stays one-sided
- spot speculation dominates market structure
- new assets lack a path to mature into fully legible markets

The industry is not broken because it failed to create open assets. It is broken because it still lacks permissionless derivatives for the long tail of tokens.
