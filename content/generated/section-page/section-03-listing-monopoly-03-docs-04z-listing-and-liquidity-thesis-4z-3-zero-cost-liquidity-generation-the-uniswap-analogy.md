## 4Z.3 “Zero-Cost” Liquidity Generation (The Uniswap Analogy)

In spot markets, **Uniswap-style AMMs** clarified what “permissionless” can mean in economic terms: users **deposit capital** into a rules-based pool, and **software** continuously prices and fills trades. Founders do not contract a bank of professional market makers to “turn on” a new pair; the protocol supplies the quoting logic. Liquidity is **generated by the mechanism**, not procured case by case—subject to fee economics, inventory risk, and adversarial trading, but not to manual MM hiring.

We use **zero-cost** here in that **institutional** sense: **no bespoke payment to a human market-making desk** to stand up liquidity for each new asset. Capital still bears risk; LPs still require returns. The claim is not that liquidity is free in aggregate, but that **marginal market creation does not require a parallel MM deal for every token**.

**Permissionless perpetuals**, if they are to match the spot analogy, imply the same shape: not only “anyone can open a perp,” but **a default path** from deposit-style participation (or an equivalent automated counterparty) to **tradeable** liquidity—until the market is mature enough for **order-book venues** to take over with natural depth.
