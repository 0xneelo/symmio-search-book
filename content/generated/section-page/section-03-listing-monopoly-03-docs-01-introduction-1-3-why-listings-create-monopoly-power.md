## 1.3 Why Listings Create Monopoly Power

Listing control creates monopoly power through several mechanisms:

**User Acquisition**
- Communities have loyal holders. To attract a community, a platform must do something for *their* token.
- Listing a community's token brings that community to the platform—along with their attention, capital, and marketing efforts.
- This is **free marketing**: communities promote platforms that list their tokens.

**Network Effects**
- More listings attract more users; more users attract more listings.
- Once a platform dominates a lifecycle stage, it becomes the default destination.
- Switching costs for users and projects increase over time.

**Gatekeeping Power**
- Listing decisions—or the lack of listing barriers—determine which tokens succeed.
- Control over listings confers influence over the entire ecosystem.
- This power can be exercised through curation, auctions, or permissionless mechanisms.

**Fee Extraction**
- Listing gatekeepers capture value at the point of exchange.
- Trading fees, listing fees, and spread capture all flow to the platform that facilitates the trade.
