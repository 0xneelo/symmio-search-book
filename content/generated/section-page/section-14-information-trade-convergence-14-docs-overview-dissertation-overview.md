## Dissertation Overview

This dissertation presents a philosophical and strategic framework for understanding the convergence of two threads running through human history: **Information** and **Trade**. At the verge of artificial general intelligence and permissionless asset creation, we argue that the only way to trust information in the future is if it is tokenized and has a market cap attached. The dissertation draws from a dialogue on the crisis of synthetic reality, the limitations of prediction markets, the missing "NO" button in the tokenized economy, and the role of permissionless perpetual derivatives as the immune system of the internet.
