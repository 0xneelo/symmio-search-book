## Abstract

Part II extends the argument of *Ode to OrderBooks* into the domain of market listings. The central claim is that order books are already the right terminal form for mature markets, but they remain incomplete as long as the path into them is selective, political, or vibes-based. The bottleneck is not execution. The bottleneck is admission.

Hyperliquid and similar systems represent progress because they push listing decisions further toward transparent mechanisms. But even improved listing mechanisms cannot serve the full scale of permissionless asset creation. They still filter assets before the market has been allowed to fully express actual derivatives demand.

Vibe solves this by creating a rule-based perpetual bootstrap layer beneath order books. A token launches, graduates to a DEX, receives a perp market on Vibe, accumulates real trader demand, and then graduates to an order book when objective thresholds are met. In this framework, Vibe becomes the discovery layer and Hyperliquid becomes the scaling layer. The result is the end of vibes-based listings and the beginning of programmatic market evolution.
