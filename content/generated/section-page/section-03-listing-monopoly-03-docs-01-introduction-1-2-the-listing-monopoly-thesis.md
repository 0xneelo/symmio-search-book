## 1.2 The Listing Monopoly Thesis

We argue that:

1. **Listings are the choke point** of the token lifecycle. Every token must pass through listing gates at multiple stages—creation, spot trading, derivatives—to achieve economic relevance.

2. **Monopoly power accrues to listing controllers.** Protocols that dominate listing at any lifecycle stage capture disproportionate value: user growth, fee revenue, network effects, and strategic influence.

3. **The token lifecycle is fragmented.** Different protocols control different stages. Binance dominates late-stage; PumpFun dominates early-stage fair launches; Uniswap dominated permissionless swapping. No single protocol yet controls the full lifecycle—but the incentive to do so is enormous.

4. **Permissionless perpetuals are the last frontier.** The progression from Bitcoin (permissionless transfer) to Uniswap (permissionless swap) to PumpFun (permissionless creation) finds its logical conclusion in permissionless derivatives. Whoever enables this completes the lifecycle and captures the corresponding monopoly position.
