# Improve Before Replacing

The most durable order-book strategy is not direct imitation. Neelo's CLOB-upgrade section says Vibe should first become useful to the order-book ecosystem.

That is a sharper claim than "Vibe also has perps." Mature central limit order books are already strong where two-sided flow, maker participation, and market history exist. Competing there first would force Vibe onto the strongest terrain of the systems it wants to complement. The better first move is upstream: discover which long-tail assets deserve deeper execution, create perp history before graduation, make shorting possible earlier, and reduce the burden on order-book venues to guess which markets matter.

The result is a cooperative posture with teeth. Vibe can help order-book ecosystems expand into more markets without asking them to lower their listing standards or support empty books. If Vibe improves the quality of assets arriving downstream, order-book communities can treat it as an enhancer rather than a weaker substitute.

## Reader Implication

When the compendium compares Vibe with order-book venues, it should avoid a venue-war frame. The first strategic question is whether Vibe can make mature execution layers stronger by preparing better markets for them.
