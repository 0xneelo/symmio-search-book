# The Token Lifecycle Concept

Neelo's token lifecycle concept starts from a simple premise: a token does not become economically relevant in one step. It moves through stages, and each stage has different listing requirements, liquidity conditions, and gatekeepers.

That framing is useful because it turns "listing" from a single event into a sequence of gates. Creation has one kind of permission structure. DEX spot access has another. CEX spot listing has another. Perpetual markets and institutional treatment have still another. A token can be open at one stage and blocked at the next.

The lifecycle concept also explains why market power is fragmented. Different protocols dominate different stages. A launchpad can control creation and graduation while a DEX controls early spot trading and a later venue controls mature derivatives. The full market is not one venue; it is a path.

## Why The Concept Matters

The concept matters for four reasons:

- power concentrates at listing gates;
- different protocols dominate different lifecycle stages;
- gaps between stages create opportunity;
- complete lifecycle control is the theoretical monopoly end state.

For Vibe, the relevant gap is not token creation or mature exchange trading. It is the missing path between early spot markets and credible derivatives access.
