# The House Of All Finance Needs A Proving Ground

The Part II conclusion turns the order-book praise into a systems claim: order books are the final layer, but they should not be forced to guess which markets deserve final-layer infrastructure.

If a venue wants to become the house of all finance, it needs more than a powerful top layer. It needs a proving ground beneath that top layer where early markets can reveal demand, survive risk, and produce evidence before admission.

## From Political Listing To Market Question

The source's strongest sentence is conceptual: Vibe turns listing from a political question into a market question.

That is the whole compendium thesis in market-structure form. Instead of asking whether a token has enough narrative force to win a listing, the system can ask whether the market has shown enough real trading behavior to move upward.

This is why Vibe's docs should not position the product as a terminal replacement for mature markets. The stronger stance is contrarian but cooperative: Vibe gives immature markets a place to prove themselves, then the mature execution layer can do what it does best.

## Reader Implication

For readers coming from Hyperliquid, Binance, dYdX, or any order-book venue, this page should make Vibe legible as upstream infrastructure. Vibe's job is not to deny order books. It is to make the eventual order-book decision less vibes-based.
