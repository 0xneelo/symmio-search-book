# Competitive Replication Checklist

The safe version of Section 8's competitive summary is a checklist: what would a competitor need to replicate before it could claim the same category?

It would need architecture that can traverse market states, not only a mature-market engine or a bootstrap vault. It would need solver capability for quote generation, inventory control, liquidation logic, hedging, refusal, monitoring, and market graduation. It would need capital design that avoids getting trapped between LP safety and mature efficiency.

It would also need data and integrations. A market-creation layer becomes more useful as it sees more markets, learns more risk profiles, and connects to launchpads, LPs, data providers, and downstream venues. That is not copied by launching a similar front end.

## What The Checklist Does Not Prove

The checklist does not prove Vibe has already won. It proves that the replication problem is broader than copying a UI or market list. The conclusion becomes defensible only if Vibe keeps executing: real markets, safe settlement, useful data, credible risk controls, and integrations that route meaningful flow.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish final superiority, competitor impossibility, specific development timelines, or investment conclusions from this page alone. The source supports a structural replication checklist; live competitive position requires current evidence.
