# The Permissionless Derivatives Thesis

Neelo's permissionless derivatives thesis extends the history of crypto infrastructure.

Bitcoin made value transfer permissionless. Ethereum made contracts permissionless. Uniswap made swapping permissionless. PumpFun-style launchpads made token creation dramatically cheaper and more open. The next logical layer is permissionless derivatives: markets that let risk transfer, leverage, hedging, and short exposure become available without a manual listing gate.

The thesis is not that derivatives are merely another trading product. Derivatives complete the expressive range of a market. Spot lets participants buy or sell the asset. Perps can add leverage, short exposure, hedging, funding, liquidation discipline, and a more demanding form of price discovery.

## Why This Belongs In Listing Monopoly

Listing control is strongest where access is scarce. Spot access became more open through DEXs, but derivative access remains comparatively gated.

That is why the permissionless derivatives thesis belongs inside a listing-monopoly argument. The unfinished permissionless frontier is not token creation or spot swapping. It is systematic derivatives access for assets that already have visible demand but no mature perp venue.

## Publication Boundary

This page supports the infrastructure-sequence thesis. It does not claim that all derivatives can be safely permissionless today, that every token is eligible for production markets, or that leverage/shorting/hedging are universally available under current Vibe policy.
