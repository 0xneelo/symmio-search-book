# Why Derivatives Matter

Spot markets let participants buy and sell ownership. Derivatives let participants express a fuller view of risk: long, short, hedge, basis trade, yield capture, or conviction that an asset deserves sustained value.

That second layer changes what a token is. Without derivatives, a token is mostly an ownership object and a narrative object. With derivatives, it becomes an object of full price discovery. The market can reward belief, punish overstatement, hedge existing holders, and discover whether demand survives leverage and short pressure.

Neelo's source makes the trader value concrete: derivatives create markets where none existed, allow hedging and arbitrage, and give traders a way to "vote no" instead of only buying or leaving. The project value is just as important. A project can turn idle inventory into market infrastructure, add token utility, create visibility, and support a market without immediately depending on a centralized listing path.

The key point is not that every token should have reckless leverage. The point is that complete market structure includes instruments for both positive and negative expression. A market that can only say yes is not finished.

## Reader Implication

When a reader asks why Vibe is about derivatives rather than only spot trading, this page should answer from first principles: derivatives are the layer that turns issuance into two-sided, stress-tested market information.
