# Market Participant Impact Map

Section 7 turns the market-lifecycle thesis into a participant map. Vibe changes the system only if the benefits and risks are legible to the people who actually use, fund, list, and route markets.

For traders, the change is access. Instead of waiting for a mature venue listing, a trader can potentially get long, short, leveraged, or hedged exposure earlier in the token lifecycle. The tradeoff is that early markets may carry wider spreads, stricter limits, and more explicit risk controls.

For LPs and market makers, the change is stage-specific opportunity. In bootstrap mode, capital earns for supporting a market before natural depth exists. As the market matures, directional exposure should decline and more traditional market-making opportunities can become possible.

For token projects, the change is agency. A project does not need to wait passively for a centralized perp listing to create derivative utility around its token. It can help a market prove itself, subject to custody, risk, disclosure, and economics review.

For exchanges and downstream venues, the change is information. A Vibe market can produce evidence about whether a listing is worth deeper infrastructure.

## Summary Table

| Participant | Source-backed impact |
| --- | --- |
| Traders | Earlier perp access, leverage, and hedging paths |
| LPs / market makers | Stage-specific yield and market-making opportunities |
| Token projects | Automatic or earlier market creation as a proof path |
| Exchanges | Better demand and risk evidence before listing |

## Publication Boundary

This page should not promise universal access, guaranteed leverage, fixed LP yield, confirmed project terms, or exchange adoption. It documents the participant-level thesis; live product behavior and economics need separate current-source confirmation.
