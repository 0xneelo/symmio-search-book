# Listing Monopoly Paper Contribution Map

The Listing Monopoly paper contributes at three levels: theoretical, practical, and strategic.

The theoretical contribution is the lifecycle lens. It treats token creation, liquidity, spot access, derivatives access, and mature venue treatment as stages where listing power can concentrate. It also uses the British Empire analogy to reason about route control, and it explains why listing control can become durable monopoly power.

The practical contribution is the market map. The paper identifies who controls which lifecycle stages, quantifies the DEX-to-perp gap as the missing stage, and analyzes how a gap-filling protocol would fit into the broader market structure.

The strategic contribution is the decision framework. It gives readers a way to evaluate listing-monopoly potential, reason about protocol design and positioning, and understand how lifecycle control could be built over time.

## Reader Implication

This page is the bridge between the paper roadmap and the detailed argument. If a reader asks what the paper adds, the answer is not only a thesis. It adds a lifecycle vocabulary, a power map, and a strategic framework for the permissionless derivatives gap.

## Publication Boundary

Treat the contribution map as an explanation of the source's intellectual work. Do not use it to publish fresh quantitative gap claims, final lifecycle-control strategy, integration commitments, or live protocol positioning without current product and market review.
