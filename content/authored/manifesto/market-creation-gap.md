# The Market Creation Gap

Neelo's market-creation argument starts with a discontinuity in the token lifecycle. A token can launch quickly, graduate into spot liquidity, and attract speculative demand, but perpetual markets usually appear only after a much later listing process. Between early spot trading and centralized perpetual availability sits the gap the compendium needs to make visible.

The gap is not only a missing product surface. It is a market-structure problem: leveraged exposure, hedging demand, and directional conviction exist before incumbent exchanges are willing to list the market. The result is delayed price discovery, opaque gatekeeping, and a long period where traders rely on substitutes instead of the market they actually want.

This is why Vibe's docs should not frame permissionless perps as "more markets" in a generic way. The stronger claim is lifecycle continuity. If derivatives can begin when demand appears, the market does not wait for a listing committee to decide that it is real enough.

## Reader Implication

The gap page should anchor the compendium's opening logic. Readers should understand that Vibe is aimed at the stage where tokens have real demand but not enough institutional permission to receive a conventional perp market.
