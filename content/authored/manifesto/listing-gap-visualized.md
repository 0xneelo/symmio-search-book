# Visualizing The Listing Lifecycle Gap

Neelo's gap diagram compresses the listing lifecycle into one vertical picture.

At the lower end, a token graduates from a launchpad-style market into DEX spot. Spot trading continues. At the higher end, mature venues can create perp markets. Between those points sits the gap: spot exists, but there is no systematic path to derivatives.

That visual is important because it separates market existence from market maturity. A token can have a pool, a chart, and holders without having the instruments that support hedging, leverage, short expression, funding, liquidation discipline, and deeper institutional participation.

## The Missing Middle

The diagram is not arguing that no one trades these tokens. It argues that the trading surface is incomplete.

In the missing middle, traders mostly have spot. Projects may have attention but not mature risk infrastructure. Venues have insufficient evidence to list derivatives confidently. The market is visible enough to matter and too immature to clear the next gate.

## Publication Boundary

The source diagram uses approximate market-cap bands and timing language. Keep the visual as a conceptual map unless those thresholds are refreshed and approved as current product or venue criteria.
