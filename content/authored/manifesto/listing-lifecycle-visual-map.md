# The Listing Lifecycle Visual Map

The source visualizes the token lifecycle as a market-cap ladder with gatekeepers at each rung.

At the bottom is creation: a token starts at launch, often through a permissionless or semi-permissionless creation surface. It then crosses a graduation rule into DEX spot trading, where platforms such as Uniswap or Raydium can support early spot liquidity.

Above that sits the gap. The source marks this as the zone where no systematic perpetual market is available. A token can have spot trading, attention, and speculation, while still lacking an instrument for hedging, shorting, leverage, or deeper derivatives-based price discovery.

Later rungs are CEX spot listing, perpetual markets, and institutional treatment. These are progressively more selective and manual. The visual is useful because it makes the missing middle obvious: crypto has open creation and open spot access, but the bridge to derivatives is not smooth.

## How To Read The Visual

Read the diagram as a control map, not a price prophecy. The thresholds organize the source's argument about market stages. The durable point is the discontinuity between early open infrastructure and later gated derivatives infrastructure.

## Publication Boundary

Do not use the diagram's market-cap numbers as current eligibility rules or product promises. Use them as a source-time visualization of lifecycle structure until refreshed market data and operator-approved listing criteria exist.
