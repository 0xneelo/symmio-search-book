# Listing Monopoly Abstract Map

Neelo's Listing Monopoly paper starts with a power map: in crypto, the platforms that decide which tokens can trade, where they trade, and when they receive deeper market structure gain unusual leverage over the whole token lifecycle.

The abstract makes three claims. First, token markets move through stages: creation, spot trading, and derivative availability. Second, each stage has gatekeepers or dominant protocols that can capture users, fees, attention, data, and strategic influence. Third, the major remaining gap is between early DEX graduation and mature perpetual market availability.

That is why the abstract matters for Vibe. The paper is not just asking for "more listings." It argues that permissionless perpetuals can become the missing lifecycle stage between fair-launch chaos and institutional market infrastructure.

## What The Abstract Contributes

The abstract contributes:

- a lifecycle frame for where market power concentrates;
- a gap between DEX-scale tokens and perp-ready tokens;
- the idea that permissionless derivatives are the last frontier after transfer, swapping, and creation became permissionless;
- a strategic lens for why Vibe should care about listing power, not only trade UI.
