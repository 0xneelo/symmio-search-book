# Curation Cost Distorts Listings

Curation cost changes what a listing venue is optimizing for.

If a venue can list freely and let markets fail, it can learn from market outcomes. If the venue bears reputational risk, support cost, liquidity cost, user-protection expectations, or capital exposure, then each bad listing has downside. The rational response is to raise the threshold before a market can exist.

That threshold reshapes behavior. Projects try to look safer, larger, more institutionally legible, more liquid, and more obviously in demand. Metrics become targets. Engagement, deposits, launch optics, and venue relationships become part of the product strategy. The market then receives assets that are easier to justify in advance, not necessarily the assets with the strongest undiscovered demand.

The source does not require bad faith to explain the distortion. Conservative curation can be rational and still reduce evolutionary discovery. A system that protects users from noise also filters out the strange early markets that do not yet have clean signals.

## Reader Implication

The compendium should frame curation as a tradeoff, not a villain. Vibe's category claim is that long-tail derivative discovery needs a lower-cost test environment before mature venues take on full listing responsibility.
