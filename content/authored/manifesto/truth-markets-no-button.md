# The Missing No Button

The "NO button" is the short side of a permissionless market: the ability to profit when a token, narrative, or information object is wrong.

Neelo's information-and-trade thesis argues that long-only markets mostly measure enthusiasm. If a participant can only buy or ignore an asset, the system has a weak way to reward skepticism. Shorting changes the game: someone with contrary information can express it with capital, and leverage can let high-conviction knowledge compete against a larger crowd of low-conviction hype.

That is why the source connects permissionless perps to verification. A market that can be shorted at launch has a built-in adversary for bad information. The claim is intentionally strong: short sellers become economically motivated fact checkers because they can make money by finding and pricing false narratives.

## Reader Implication

This page should be used to explain the ideological weight behind "perps for any token." It is not only market access. It is the addition of a negative vote with capital behind it.
