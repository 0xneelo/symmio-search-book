# Proof Of Value Future Directions

Neelo's conclusion makes the Proof of Value paper more useful by naming what remains to be tested.

The first future direction is empirical validation. Post-launch evidence should include LP yield data, trader volume and retention, project participation, revenue distribution, and observed stress outcomes during volatile periods. Those are not ornamental metrics. They are the difference between a thesis and an operating record.

The second direction is theoretical extension. The source calls for more formal work on capital efficiency, revenue-share calibration, and cross-market risk correlation. That matters because long-tail perp sustainability is not a single-market problem. A system can appear safe one market at a time while still accumulating correlated stress across many young markets.

The third direction is integration. The Proof of Value framework should be connected to the bootstrap trilemma, the listing-monopoly lifecycle position, and the mathematical derivation work. In compendium form, that means the conclusion is not an ending. It is a map of which evidence pages, formulas, and market reports need to mature next.

## Living-Docs Implication

This page should feed the gaps queue. If readers ask for live LP yield, stress-test outcomes, revenue-share calibration, or cross-market risk, the docs should not answer with source-time theory. They should either route to current evidence or create an explicit gap.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish live LP yields, trader retention, project revenue distributions, stress-test outcomes, optimal revenue-share settings, cross-market correlation models, or mathematical-finality claims without fresh primary-source/operator/product/risk/legal/accounting review.
