# Lifecycle Gap Summary

Neelo's lifecycle-gap summary reduces Section 4 to one claim: the distance from DEX graduation to perp availability is a structural inefficiency in crypto markets.

That inefficiency disconnects early fair-launch and DEX ecosystems from later institutional infrastructure. It leaves spot markets to carry speculative demand without the derivative instruments that support hedging, shorting, leverage, and richer price discovery.

The summary also sets up the next move in the paper. If the gap is real, then the next question is not just whether markets can be listed. The question is whether a protocol can turn an early listing into a tradeable, measured, maturing derivatives market.

## Bridge To Section 5

Section 4 diagnoses the gap. Section 4Z sharpens the liquidity requirement. Section 5 asks what a permissionless perp protocol would need to do if it tried to fill the missing lifecycle stage.

That sequence matters for the compendium. It prevents the reader from mistaking permissionless perps for a symbol-creation feature. The thesis is lifecycle continuity plus bootstrap liquidity.

## Publication Boundary

Use this as a source-backed summary of Neelo's lifecycle-gap argument. Do not treat it as final evidence that any current product has fully solved the gap, integrated with later venues, or created approved graduation paths.
