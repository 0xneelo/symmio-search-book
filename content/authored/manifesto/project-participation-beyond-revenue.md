# Project Participation Is Not Only Revenue

The trader/project value source says project participation is not reducible to a fee-share offer.

A project may support a Vibe-style market because it gives the token utility, gives holders a hedging and speculation surface, creates visibility, signals seriousness to the community, and turns idle inventory into market infrastructure. Revenue share can strengthen the deal, but the source's stronger point is alignment: the market is valuable to the project even before it is treated as a pure yield product.

That matters for documentation. If the docs sell participation only as "deposit tokens, earn fees," they make the market sound like a passive vault. The better framing is market formation. A project is helping create a derivatives surface for its own asset while accepting bounded incremental exposure on inventory it already holds.

## The Safer Public Framing

The source includes example economics and partner-context claims. Public docs should avoid turning those into live guarantees until reviewed. The publishable principle is still useful:

Project-side token inventory can be rational when it adds utility, market access, price discovery, community signaling, and treasury productivity without requiring the project to spend scarce stablecoins upfront.

## Publication Boundary

Keep final revenue-share rates, partner counts, market eligibility, vault rights, deposit minimums, withdrawal terms, treasury-accounting treatment, and live launch commitments under operator, legal, accounting, and implementation review.
