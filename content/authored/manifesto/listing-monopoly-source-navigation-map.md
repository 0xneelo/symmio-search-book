# Listing Monopoly Source Navigation Map

Neelo's Listing Monopoly overview, 4Z summary, acknowledgments, references, and version-history fragments are source-navigation material for the token-lifecycle thesis. They should route readers into the authored paper spine, not become eight separate public pages.

The overview fragments state the paper's job: explain why control over market listings at each token-lifecycle stage becomes industry power. Route readers who need the paper sequence to `authored-listing-monopoly-paper-roadmap`, the compressed thesis to `authored-listing-monopoly-abstract-map`, and the lifecycle-control claim to `authored-listing-lifecycle-thesis-statement`.

The table-of-contents and reading-order fragments are audience routing. Executives should start with the abstract, introduction, and conclusion. Competitive readers should use the framework, landscape, and Thiel analysis. Builders and partners should focus on the lifecycle gap, listing-plus-liquidity, the hypothetical permissionless perps model, and strategic implications.

The key-theme fragments preserve the source's hierarchy: listing control, lifecycle evolution, the DEX-to-perp gap, listing plus liquidity, and the low-cap perp wedge. Route lifecycle questions to `authored-listing-lifecycle-stage-map`, landscape questions to `authored-listing-controller-landscape-overview`, liquidity questions to `authored-listing-plus-liquidity-thesis`, and wedge questions to `authored-listing-thiel-small-market-wedge`.

The 4Z summary is the corrective: permissionless listing alone is not the thesis. A symbol with no robust liquidity path is not a useful market. Route that claim to `authored-listing-plus-liquidity-thesis`, generalized counterparty questions to `authored-listing-generalized-market-maker-problem`, and flywheel questions to `authored-listing-liquidity-listings-flywheel`.

The acknowledgments, references, and version-history fragments are publication-boundary signals. They name source-time review needs: Binance community-listing history, PumpFun market-share and graduation data, Uniswap volume and market share, Hyperliquid HIP-3 documentation, and research on bootstrap architectures. Do not publish those placeholders as settled, current quantitative claims.
