# The NO Button As Market Filter

The source's fake-video scenario is a stress test for market filtering. A synthetic claim appears. Coordinated buyers push a token or narrative upward. The market briefly treats attention as reality.

The NO button changes the filter. A participant who recognizes the claim as false can do more than ignore it. They can short it, adding capital-weighted rejection to the price process. If their evidence is right and the market reprices downward, the false claim loses value and the skeptic is paid for the correction.

This is not a claim that every market price becomes objective truth. It is a claim that a two-sided market has a stronger filter than a one-sided market. The positive side measures belief. The negative side tests whether belief survives confrontation.

Docs should translate the source's scenario into mechanism: short-side access, leverage limits, solver pricing, inventory, margin, and settlement. The user-facing metaphor is a button; the real filter is the market stack under it.

## Reader Implication

The NO button is the product phrase for capitalized rejection. It filters narratives by letting informed skeptics act inside the market rather than outside it.
