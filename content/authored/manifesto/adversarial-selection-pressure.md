# Adversarial Selection Pressure

The source uses predator language to describe what short sellers add to a market. Publication prose should translate that into adversarial selection pressure.

In a hype-only environment, weak claims can survive if attention keeps arriving. A shortable market adds actors who actively search for fragile narratives, bad incentives, fake AI media, scams, or vaporware because finding them can be profitable. They are not necessarily altruists. Their selfish incentive can still make overstatement more expensive.

This is the evolutionary logic behind the NO button. A healthy market needs pressure from both sides. Believers fund the positive case. Skeptics test the negative case. Solvers and risk controls decide what can be priced. Claims that cannot survive that pressure lose market credibility faster.

The source's "assassination markets for lies" phrasing is intentionally provocative. The public compendium should not glorify harm or vandalism. The safer mechanism is market selection: false or overhyped claims should face earlier economic pressure, while user risk, market integrity, and abuse controls stay explicit.

## Reader Implication

Adversarial selection pressure explains why Vibe's negative side is not just a sentiment feature. It is a market discipline layer designed to make cheap falsehood face expensive opposition.
