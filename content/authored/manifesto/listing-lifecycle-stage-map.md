# Listing Monopoly Lifecycle Stage Map

Neelo's lifecycle map divides a token's path into six stages: creation, DEX spot trading, the valley, CEX spot listing, perpetual markets, and institutional scale.

In the creation stage, a token launches through a bonding curve or similar mechanism. The launch can be permissionless, but graduation becomes the first gate: when and how does the token move from creation into a DEX market?

In the DEX spot stage, a graduated token receives AMM-based spot trading. This stage is more open than centralized listing because a token can trade once the relevant liquidity and pool mechanics exist. Liquidity quality still varies widely.

The valley is the gap. A token has shown some market interest, but systematic perpetual access does not exist. That is where the source's listing-monopoly argument becomes strategically important: the market has demand signals, but not the derivative infrastructure needed for two-sided expression.

CEX spot and perpetual stages become progressively more selective. Committees, market-cap thresholds, volume expectations, market-maker requirements, venue discretion, and opaque listing logic become more important. Institutional or ETF-stage treatment is outside the paper's main scope, but it represents the terminal end of the lifecycle.

## Publication Boundary

The source uses market-cap thresholds and market-share examples as a strategic map. Treat those as source-time illustrations unless refreshed. Do not publish exact current thresholds, PumpFun share, Uniswap share, CEX listing policies, token counts, or venue requirements without updated primary evidence.
