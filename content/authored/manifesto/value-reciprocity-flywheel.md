# The Value Reciprocity Flywheel

Neelo's trader/project value source ends with a reciprocity loop: more projects deposit inventory, more markets exist, more traders arrive, more fee flow appears, more LP revenue becomes possible, and more projects are attracted.

That loop is useful, but only if the docs state its condition: it depends on structural alignment. If projects provide inventory but traders get poor execution, the loop breaks. If traders arrive but winners cannot be paid, the loop breaks. If LPs or projects bear hidden tail risk, the loop breaks. If the solver cannot price and hedge residual exposure, the loop breaks.

The flywheel is therefore not a growth slogan. It is a test of whether the system assigns value and risk coherently across projects, token holders, traders, solvers, and the protocol.

## How The Compendium Should Use It

The reciprocity loop belongs near Proof of Value because it explains why Vibe is more than a listing interface. The product has to create feedback between market creation and market evidence:

- inventory makes a market possible;
- trading creates price and demand signals;
- fee flow tests whether the market is economically real;
- reporting shows whether the market is sustainable;
- successful markets can mature toward deeper venue integrations.

That is the bridge from bootstrap to proof. A market that cannot sustain the loop should not be dressed up as validated.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish the flywheel as an assurance that every market will attract traders, generate yield, or graduate. Live eligibility, reporting thresholds, revenue allocation, market-retirement rules, and graduation criteria remain operator, risk, legal, accounting, and implementation review.
