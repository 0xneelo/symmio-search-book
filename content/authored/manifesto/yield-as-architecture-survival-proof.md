# Yield As Architecture Survival Proof

In the Vibe Pillars source, yield is not a marketing surface. It is survival proof.

Once an early market relies on a solver, market maker, vault, or designated residual counterparty, that capital is taking risk. It may warehouse inventory, fund stable collateral, support quotes, or absorb one-sided exposure while the market is immature.

If the return on that capital is too weak for the risk, the architecture does not survive. Capital leaves, spreads widen, capacity shrinks, and the market becomes technically possible but economically unavailable.

## The Risk-Adjusted Question

The relevant question is not "can LPs earn yield?" It is whether they can earn enough risk-adjusted yield for thin, volatile, adverse-selection-prone markets.

That ties the third pillar back to the first two. A safe system that cannot pay risk-takers will not scale. A bootstrappable system that consumes too much idle capital per market will not persist.

## Publication Boundary

This page documents the architectural claim from Neelo's source. It does not publish final Vibe LP rates, fee routing, solver compensation, vault rights, or Phase B economics. Those remain operator/accounting/legal/risk/implementation review items.
