# One Counterparty For Niche Verification

The Information/Trade source makes one of its cleanest capital-efficiency claims in the no-pool section: a niche claim does not need a deep liquidity pool before anyone can test it. It needs a counterparty willing to take the other side.

That distinction is central to long-tail markets. If every local news event, small token, narrow narrative, or early project needs a fully seeded AMM, most possible markets will never start. Capital is too scarce, and each pool fragments liquidity before demand is proven.

An intent-based OTC design changes the minimum viable market. A trader signs an intent. A solver or counterparty evaluates it. If the risk can be priced, a bilateral trade can exist. The first trade does not prove the claim is true. It proves at least one informed counterparty was willing to price the disagreement.

This is a thesis page, not a production promise that Vibe will list every fact, video, local event, or information market. Exact supported assets, categories, eligibility rules, and dispute or settlement semantics need product and implementation review.

## Reader Implication

When readers ask why Vibe can address the long tail, the answer is not "because pools are unnecessary forever." The answer is narrower: the first credible price can come from a counterparty relationship before a market has earned deep pooled liquidity.
