# Leverage As Truth Amplifier

The missing-no-button thesis is not only about shorting. It is also about force.

Neelo's source argues that truth may sit with a minority: one expert, one skeptical trader, one participant who has better information than a larger crowd. In a long-only or one-dollar-one-vote market, that minority can be overwhelmed by broader low-conviction hype. Leverage changes the expression of conviction. A smaller participant with stronger evidence can apply more market pressure than their cash balance alone would allow.

That is the philosophical claim behind "permissionless shorting with leverage." The source wants capital to punish falsehood faster than social commentary can. In product docs, this has to be translated carefully. Leverage is also risk. The compendium should explain the mechanism without implying that live Vibe markets will expose unlimited leverage or identical limits across every asset.

## Publication Boundary

Exact leverage ceilings, eligible assets, margin rules, liquidation rules, and user-facing risk warnings must come from current product and protocol sources. This page documents the thesis: leverage can amplify high-conviction negative information, but only a controlled market design can make that useful instead of destructive.
