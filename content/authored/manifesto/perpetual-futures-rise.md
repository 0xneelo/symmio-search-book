# The Rise Of Perpetual Futures

Neelo's introduction starts from a simple market fact: perpetual futures became the crypto derivative primitive because they give continuous leveraged exposure without an expiry date.

The mechanism matters. A traditional future expires. A perpetual keeps the contract alive and uses funding to keep the perp price anchored to the underlying spot market. That turns the product into ongoing market infrastructure for speculation, hedging, arbitrage, and yield-seeking liquidity.

The paper's first move is therefore not "Vibe needs more markets." It is "perps are already central, but new perp market creation remains unsolved." Mature venues can process large flows once a market exists. The harder question is what happens before there is a liquid book, reliable two-sided flow, and enough market evidence to justify listing.

## Publication Boundary

The source includes source-time examples for daily volume, spot-versus-perp ratios, open interest, and Hyperliquid activity. Do not publish those numbers as current without fresh market-data verification. The durable claim is the product-structure claim: no-expiry perps became core crypto market infrastructure, which makes the missing market-creation layer strategically important.
