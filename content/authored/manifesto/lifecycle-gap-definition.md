# Defining The Lifecycle Gap

The lifecycle gap is the interval between DEX graduation and perpetual market availability.

That gap is not a small UX inconvenience. It affects market structure, capital formation, hedging, short expression, leverage, and the way crypto infrastructure decides which assets deserve mature trading rails.

A token can leave its launch environment, develop spot liquidity, attract attention, and still have no credible perp market. It may be too large to treat as pure creation-stage speculation, but too early for Binance, Hyperliquid, or another mature venue to list with deep liquidity and operational confidence.

## Why The Definition Matters

The definition gives the Listing Monopoly thesis its target.

If the gap is real, the strategic question becomes: who creates the missing derivatives layer for tokens in transition? The answer cannot be only "more DEX pools," because spot access already exists. It cannot be only "wait for CEXs or order books," because that preserves the late-stage bottleneck.

Vibe's thesis sits in the middle: create an earlier perp surface that can test demand, reveal market quality, and help markets mature before they qualify for the deepest venues.

## Publication Boundary

The source defines the gap conceptually. Exact market-cap bands, token eligibility, launch criteria, graduation thresholds, and live Vibe coverage require operator/product/risk/legal review before becoming public product promises.
