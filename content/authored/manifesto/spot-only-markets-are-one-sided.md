# Spot-Only Markets Are One-Sided

Spot markets are necessary, but they do not give a token complete market expression. They mostly let participants buy, sell existing holdings, or leave.

That one-sidedness explains many familiar crypto symptoms: narratives can outrun fundamentals, attention can substitute for conviction, and token markets can look active before they have been shorted, hedged, or stress-tested. The market has a yes button, but the no side is weak.

Derivatives add the missing market language. They let participants short, hedge, basis trade, and express risk without requiring them to hold or sell the underlying token directly. That does not guarantee truth, but it makes overstatement more expensive and skepticism more useful.

## Reader Implication

The docs should not present perps as a casino layer pasted onto tokens. The better explanation is that derivatives complete the market grammar. A token market that can only express enthusiasm is not done.
