# The Lifecycle Gap As Product Surface

The lifecycle gap is the product opening for Vibe. Neelo's papers describe a token path where spot markets can appear quickly, but derivative markets arrive late, manually, and only for a narrow set of assets. Vibe's opportunity is to turn that missing interval into a product surface.

The conventional lifecycle has a blank space between DEX liquidity and major-venue derivatives. Vibe's lifecycle fills that space with a market that can begin early, survive only if real flow appears, and graduate toward more efficient structure when the evidence supports it.

This is a sharper product story than "permissionless listings." Permissionless listing sounds like a supply-side action: create a market. Lifecycle coverage is demand-side: let traders, hedgers, solvers, and LPs reveal whether the market deserves to mature.

## Reader Implication

This page should sit near the bridge between manifesto and product docs. It gives the user a reason to care about dashboard data, volume, solver economics, referrals, and source-linked questions: all of those surfaces are evidence that a market is moving through its lifecycle.
