# Listing Without Liquidity

Neelo's Section 4Z separates instrument existence from market quality.

A venue can list a perpetual, allow broad market creation, or accept user-driven proposals, but each new instrument still needs someone to quote it. Until makers provide depth, the market is listed in name while remaining thin, wide, or empty in practice.

## The Split That Matters

The source's central distinction is that listing scales more easily than quoting. Adding a symbol is an administrative or technical act. Making that symbol tradeable requires capital, risk management, incentives, and participant coordination.

That is why "permissionless listing" is incomplete when it produces a graveyard of empty books. The category-defining problem is permissionless listing paired with generalized liquidity generation.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish current venue-specific listing rules, maker incentives, governance paths, liquidity depth, or dYdX-style market-quality claims without fresh primary-source/operator/product/market review.
