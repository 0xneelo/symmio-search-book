# The No-Ponzi Market Revenue Test

Neelo's sustainability section gives the compendium a useful test: does yield come from real market activity, or does it depend on new deposits?

That question matters for any LP-facing market, but it matters even more for long-tail perps. Early deposits can make a market look healthy before traders arrive. Partner counts can signal demand before revenue exists. A market can show large inventory and still fail the economic test if fees, spread, funding, and liquidation economics never materialize into durable revenue.

The source's answer is lifecycle-based. In bootstrap, LP deposits ignite the market and the solver carries more residual responsibility. In maturity, more two-sided flow can net trader-to-trader, spreads can tighten, solver exposure can decline, and the market can become a better graduation candidate. The healthier path is not endless deposit growth. It is declining dependence on subsidy and increasing evidence of real flow.

## What To Measure

The no-ponzi test should become part of the living-docs measurement agenda:

- Is revenue coming from trading activity rather than new LP deposits?
- Are spreads and funding compensating actual risk?
- Is solver residual exposure declining as markets mature?
- Are LPs paid from repeatable market economics?
- Do stressed markets use the disclosed defense stack before ADL?

If the docs track those questions, Proof of Value becomes falsifiable rather than purely rhetorical.

## Publication Boundary

Current live market revenue, LP payouts, solver profitability, and stress outcomes need operator-approved source data before final publication. This page documents the test the compendium should apply.
