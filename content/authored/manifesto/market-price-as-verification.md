# Market Price As Verification Layer

Proof of Value starts from a verification crisis: information is cheap to generate, so the cost of producing a claim no longer proves anything. Neelo's answer is that commitment becomes the filter. A claim exposed to market price has a different status from a claim that only circulates as content.

The source contrasts this with prediction-market resolution. A binary market needs a resolver and therefore works best for bounded public questions. The long tail of tokenized claims, memes, projects, and synthetic realities needs a continuous signal. In that framing, price action is not a final court judgment, but it is an always-on measure of capitalized belief.

Vibe enters because a long-tail verification layer needs both sides. Spot markets let supporters buy. Perps and intents let skeptics express the negative side, hedge, or quote a counterview without requiring a full AMM liquidity pool for every niche claim.

## Reader Implication

This is the philosophical reason the compendium spends so much space on shorting, solvers, and market creation. Without a negative side, markets become attention amplifiers. With a negative side and capital at risk, they become a harsher verification mechanism.
