# Pre-Revenue Commitment Is A Signal, Not Proof

Neelo's validation section treats launch-partner commitment before full product maturity as meaningful evidence. The compendium should preserve that signal, but it should not confuse it with final proof.

Pre-revenue commitment can say several useful things. It can show that projects understand the problem Vibe is solving. It can show that token holders and treasuries value an alternative to stablecoin-heavy listing paths. It can show that the project-side offer is legible before payouts, historical yields, or permissionless listing workflows are fully observable.

But it cannot prove that the market will be sustainable. A project can commit inventory because the proposition is attractive and still discover later that trader flow is weak, solver costs are high, spreads are too wide, or stress behavior is worse than expected. Partner count is demand evidence, not a substitute for market evidence.

## How To Use The Signal

The docs should use pre-revenue commitment as an early validation layer. It belongs before operational metrics, not after them.

The sequence is:

- projects commit because the alternative-cost stack is painful;
- markets launch and produce actual trader behavior;
- trader behavior produces fees, funding, liquidations, solver exposure, and support questions;
- the living-docs and reporting layer show which markets are maturing and which remain subsidized or stressed.

That framing lets the compendium stay honest. Early partner interest matters because sophisticated projects rarely commit inventory to a proposition they do not understand. Sustainable proof arrives later, through repeated market use and stress outcomes.
