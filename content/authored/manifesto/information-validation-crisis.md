# The Information Validation Crisis

The Part II thesis starts before derivatives. Neelo's source frames civilization as a sequence of better information systems: speech, writing, printing, computers, the internet, and now AI. Each step lowers the cost of producing, preserving, moving, and recombining information.

The current break is that generation has become cheap enough to overwhelm validation. AI pushes markets into a world where convincing content can be produced faster than communities can verify it. The bottleneck is no longer expression. The bottleneck is deciding what deserves belief, capital, attention, and risk.

That is why the compendium should treat markets as information infrastructure. A market is not only a place to speculate. It is a mechanism for forcing claims to carry cost. Spot buying expresses belief, but it is asymmetric: it mostly rewards enthusiasm. Derivatives add the missing side of information by letting participants hedge, short, and pressure-test claims.

This is the bridge from Proof of Value to Vibe. If the world can create infinite claims and infinite tokens, the serious infrastructure question becomes: which systems can validate them without central gatekeepers?

## Reader Implication

New readers should understand that Vibe's thesis is not "more trading" for its own sake. It is a validation thesis: markets become more useful when they can express both belief and skepticism around any asset that earns attention.
