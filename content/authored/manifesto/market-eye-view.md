# The Market's Eye View

The market's eye view is the idea that a claim can be evaluated by continuous market pressure instead of waiting for a final external resolver.

Prediction markets often need someone or something to say whether a question resolved yes or no. Neelo's Proof of Value source argues that this model does not scale to the long tail of tokenized claims because each resolved question needs setup, boundaries, and a closing judgment.

The alternative is the market's eye view. A claim does not wait for a judge. It trades continuously. Price action, liquidity, survival, and collapse become the running evaluation. The source frames this as a shift from bounded betting to ownership-like market pressure: instead of asking whether an event resolves, the market asks whether a claim can sustain value over time.

## Mechanism

A market's eye view is not a God's eye view. It is not objective omniscience. It is the continuously updated view of participants willing to risk capital.

That distinction matters because a temporary pump is not proof. The stronger signal is persistence under adversarial pressure: can the claim hold value, attract liquidity, survive rejection, and keep trading after the initial narrative shock?

## Why It Matters For Vibe

Vibe matters because continuous market verification needs a negative side. Without shorting, the market mostly measures buy-side enthusiasm. With permissionless derivatives, skeptics can attack, hedge, or refuse claims directly. That makes the market's eye more adversarial and more useful.

The public claim should stay bounded: Vibe can add a two-sided verification surface around tokenized claims; it should not be described as producing final truth or guaranteeing that price equals reality.
