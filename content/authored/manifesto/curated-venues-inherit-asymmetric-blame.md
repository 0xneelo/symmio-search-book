# Curated Venues Inherit Asymmetric Blame

Curated listing systems do not only filter markets. They inherit responsibility for the quality of what passes through the filter.

Neelo's curation-cost section makes this incentive clear. If users believe a venue screens assets, they also expect some protection from obviously bad listings. When a listed token collapses, manipulates users, or proves unserious, blame flows back toward the venue that appeared to endorse it. That reputational downside is asymmetric: a venue may receive limited credit for a good listing, but can receive outsized blame for a bad one.

The rational response is conservatism. Venues raise thresholds, require more proof, add deposits, demand operational readiness, avoid weird assets, and prefer tokens that already look institutionally legible. That can protect users in one dimension while weakening discovery in another.

## Not A Moral Failure

This page should keep the argument disciplined. Curated venues are not automatically malicious or obsolete. They are solving a different optimization problem. If a venue bears reputational, capital, support, or legal cost, it has to protect itself against being wrong.

The problem is that this protection changes what gets discovered. Markets with high perceived interest get more access. Markets with uncertain but potentially high actual interest are delayed or excluded. Projects learn that appearing safe may matter more than letting demand reveal itself.

Vibe's role is not to make curation disappear. It is to create an earlier test environment where the market can generate evidence before mature venues accept the asymmetric blame of deeper listing.
