# Architecture Defined By The Constraint Set

Neelo's conclusion reframes Vibe away from a simple listing story. Vibe is not defined only by what it lists. It is defined by the constraint set it tries to solve.

The source names three required conditions: the market must be hard to exploit, tradable before continuous two-sided flow exists, and economically attractive enough for risk capital to stay. Each pillar is necessary. None is sufficient alone.

## Why This Framing Matters

If the docs describe Vibe only as "permissionless perps for more tokens," the architecture can sound arbitrary. Readers may miss why the product needs solvers, risk controls, funding, vaults, insurance boundaries, and staged market maturity.

The constraint-set framing makes the design legible. Low-cap perps are difficult because leverage, thin flow, and capital incentives collide. Vibe's architecture is the answer to that collision, not a loose bundle of features.

## Publication Boundary

This page supports the strategic architecture thesis. It does not finalize listing eligibility, solver obligations, vault exposure, insurance policy, graduation criteria, or live market parameters.
