# Ode To OrderBooks Part II Source Navigation Map

Neelo's Ode to OrderBooks Part II abstract, overview, table-of-contents, core-thesis, and source-marker fragments are source-navigation material for the listing-admission argument. They should route readers into the authored Part II pages, not become six separate public pages.

Part II sharpens the first Ode thesis: order books are still the mature execution layer, but listing admission remains incomplete when venues must choose markets through selective, political, auctioned, or vibes-based signals. Route admission questions to `authored-order-book-admission-bottleneck` and narrative-listing questions to `authored-end-of-narrative-based-listings`.

The Hyperliquid-facing thread is that HIP-3-style builder-deployed perps are progress, but a top-layer order book still needs a lower discovery layer for assets that have not yet produced derivatives demand. Route that claim to `authored-hyperliquid-gap-lower-layer`, and keep any integration or handoff wording under current source review.

The assembly-line thread is the source's operating metaphor: launchpad to DEX to Vibe perp to order book. Route lifecycle-path questions to `authored-launchpad-dex-vibe-orderbook-path`, market-evidence questions to `authored-graduation-data-checklist`, and promotion-rule questions to `authored-programmatic-market-graduation`.

The source-marker fragments name transcript provenance. Treat them as traceability, not as production guarantees. The public docs can publish the architectural relationship between discovery and mature execution, but not automatic graduation thresholds, confirmed venue integrations, or current listing policy.

## Publication Boundary

HIP-3 outcomes, direct Hyperliquid routing, automatic graduation rules, maturity thresholds, market-score formulas, launchpad partnerships, order-book listing policy, admission automation, and any claim that Vibe can end vibes-based listings in production require fresh primary-source, product, market, risk, legal, and operator review before publication as current facts.
