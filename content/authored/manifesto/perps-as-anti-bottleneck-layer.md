# Perps As An Anti-Bottleneck Layer

The game-theory paper ends with a structural claim: listing design is a theory of market evolution.

Crypto made token creation radically permissionless, but serious market access remains scarce. A token can be created quickly, traded on a DEX, and still have no serious path to shorting, hedging, leverage, or institutional liquidity. That creates a bottleneck between issuance abundance and market validation.

Neelo's source calls Vibe strategically important because perps should not stay trapped inside the same listing logic as centralized exchanges and high-cost curated venues. If derivative markets only arrive after a token is obvious, then they mostly confirm what the market already selected. They do less to discover what is still uncertain.

## The Anti-Bottleneck Claim

Vibe is an anti-bottleneck layer when it lets derivative access arrive earlier in the lifecycle without requiring a mature venue to endorse the asset first.

That has a precise meaning. It does not say every market will be high quality. It says the market can test, hedge, short, and price assets before a centralized authority or order-book venue is comfortable listing them. The resulting evidence can then help downstream venues, solvers, projects, and communities reason about actual demand.

This is why the compendium should frame Vibe as market infrastructure, not just an exchange with more tickers. The hard product is not a symbol list. It is a defended path from permissionless token creation to permissionless derivative discovery.
