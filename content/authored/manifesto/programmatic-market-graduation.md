# Programmatic Market Graduation

Part II of Ode to OrderBooks turns the complementarity thesis into a listing thesis: markets should move upward because they have matured, not because someone liked their story.

Once Vibe exists as a discovery layer, a downstream listing can be based on observed market evidence rather than perceived interest. The source names the relevant categories: sustained perp volume, trader count, liquidity depth, healthy liquidation behavior, two-sided participation, and declining need for solver support.

That is programmatic graduation. The market earns its next execution layer by showing that it can handle more efficient structure.

## Why It Matters

Programmatic graduation reduces the listing committee's guessing burden. The order-book venue no longer has to infer demand from narrative alone. It can read a market history: who traded, how often, with what spread, under what volatility, and with what risk profile.

This gives Vibe's answer engine a clean way to explain market maturity: early markets are not failed order books; they are markets being tested for whether an order book should exist.

## Publication Boundary

The source gives categories of graduation criteria, not confirmed live Vibe thresholds. Final docs need product confirmation for formulas, cadence, qualifying venues, and whether any graduation process is automated.
