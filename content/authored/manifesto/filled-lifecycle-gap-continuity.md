# What Changes If The Lifecycle Gap Is Filled

If permissionless perps become available shortly after DEX graduation, the token lifecycle stops having an empty middle.

The source's proposed path is:

creation -> graduation -> DEX spot -> permissionless perps -> maturation -> order-book or CEX perps -> institutional or ETF-style endpoints.

The main idea is continuity. Early token ecosystems and late institutional infrastructure would no longer be separated by a long interval where spot can trade but derivatives cannot form.

## The Reader-Level Implication

Continuity changes how market participants read a token's progress.

A project can move from launch to spot to derivative evidence without waiting for a mature exchange gate. A trader can express long, short, leveraged, or hedged views earlier. A downstream venue can evaluate real market quality instead of only narrative, spot volume, and community heat.

The filled-gap lifecycle does not remove later venues. It gives markets a more informative route toward them.

## Publication Boundary

This page explains a source model. It does not confirm current automatic lifecycle routing, market graduation, institutional access, ETF outcomes, or downstream integrations.
