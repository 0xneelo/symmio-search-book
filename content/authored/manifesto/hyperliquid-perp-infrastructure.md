# Hyperliquid As Perp Infrastructure

Neelo uses Hyperliquid to show how permissionless listing can move closer to derivatives while still serving a later lifecycle stage.

The source frames Hyperliquid as high-performance perp infrastructure: a custom L1, on-chain order book, and large daily volume at source time. Its importance is not just performance. It also shows that perp listing is becoming more programmable.

## HIP-3 As A Listing Mechanism

The source reads HIP-3 as a permissionless listing mechanism with a financial barrier. Anyone can bid, but not every project can afford to win or sustain the market. That makes HIP-3 more open than a purely manual committee process, yet still biased toward tokens with capital, community, and enough established demand to justify the listing effort.

This is why Neelo places Hyperliquid near Binance in the late-stage part of the lifecycle. The form of access changes, but order-book markets still need liquidity, depth, maker participation, and credible demand.

## Spot Markets And The Middle

Hyperliquid also has spot markets, but the source keeps the same lifecycle distinction. Smaller projects may find DEX spot easier because a pool can exist without the same listing-auction, market-maker, or depth requirements.

The strategic point is not that Hyperliquid is irrelevant. It is that high-performance order-book infrastructure does not automatically fill the whole gap between DEX graduation and mature perp availability.
