# CLOB Majors-First Stage Fit

The listing annex gives order books their strongest form before criticizing their long-tail limits. Central limit order book perps are excellent when both sides already want to trade, when makers are present, and when the asset is watched closely enough for tight spreads and depth to persist.

That is why CLOBs are majors-first infrastructure. They are not weak because they are order books. They are weak at the moment before a market has earned the conditions that make an order book feel liquid.

## What Has To Exist First

A CLOB needs more than a symbol:

- makers who believe quoting size will earn enough;
- traders who believe entry and exit will be usable;
- price anchors that make marks legible;
- enough two-sided interest to avoid stale or one-sided books.

For majors, those conditions can already be present. For long-tail assets, they often are not. The market may need a discovery and bootstrap layer before it deserves mature CLOB treatment.

## Publication Boundary

This page should not publish live venue-specific listing cadence, maker-program terms, liquidity-mining rewards, or market-count claims without rechecking current third-party docs. The durable source-backed point is stage fit: CLOBs are strong after liquidity is already coordinated.
