# Proof Of Value Capture Mechanisms

The capture side of the Thiel analysis asks how a useful market becomes an economically durable system.

The source names several potential capture mechanisms: trading fees, solver spread revenue, protocol or management share, data and analytics products, and graduation or integration paths with downstream venues. The useful documentation move is to keep those mechanisms separate. They do not have the same source, risk, timing, or durability.

Trading fees depend on real volume. Solver spread depends on pricing skill, hedge access, and risk controls. Protocol share depends on public economics and implementation. Data products require enough markets to produce reliable signals. Graduation or integration economics depend on venue relationships and market maturity.

## Capture Is Not One Number

The compendium should avoid collapsing all of this into "revenue." A trader reading the docs needs to know which costs affect them. A project needs to know what share, custody, or service terms actually apply. A solver or LP needs to know which economics compensate which risks. An operator needs to know which numbers are live, planned, configurable, or agreement-specific.

The source's strongest contribution is the architecture logic: capture should come from coordinating market creation, not from quietly worsening participant outcomes.

## Publication Boundary

Protocol share, solver spread revenue, management fees, data products, graduation fees, and venue partnerships are not automatically public facts just because they appear in the strategy source. Publish them only with current primary-source/operator/accounting/legal approval and formula versioning.
