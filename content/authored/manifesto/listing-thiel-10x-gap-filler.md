# Listing Monopoly 10x Gap-Filler Claim

The Listing Monopoly Thiel source uses the 10x technology test to distinguish a real category from a marginal feature.

The claimed improvement is not a nicer interface for assets that already have deep perps. It is the creation of a derivatives market where the current alternative is often no market at all, a manual wait for a mature venue, or a bootstrap design that cannot scale across the long tail.

## What 10x Means Here

In this source, "10x" means capability, speed, and market access. A gap-filling layer can be 10x better if it makes early perps possible before order books can bootstrap them, before centralized venues can justify listing them, and without reducing permissionless listing to empty ticker creation.

That last distinction ties back to Section 4Z. The hard problem is not symbolic listing. It is tradeable market creation with generalized liquidity support.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish final 10x superiority, supported market count, live liquidity sufficiency, solver performance, or current product capability as settled facts without fresh market/product/operator/risk evidence. The source-backed claim is that a gap-filling architecture is designed to enable a market state incumbents do not currently serve.
