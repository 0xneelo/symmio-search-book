# The Evolution Of Permissionless Infrastructure

The Listing Monopoly introduction places permissionless perpetuals in a sequence of crypto infrastructure breakthroughs.

Bitcoin made value transfer permissionless. Ethereum made programmable value permissionless. Uniswap made swapping permissionless. PumpFun made token creation dramatically easier and more open. Neelo's next step is permissionless derivatives: the ability to create two-sided derivative markets for long-tail assets without waiting for a conventional listing gate.

The sequence matters because each phase removed a barrier. First users could transfer value without permission. Then developers could program value. Then traders could swap without a centralized listing. Then creators could launch tokens with lower friction. But derivatives still remain a harder gate: shorting, hedging, leverage, and mature price discovery are not available for most assets at creation.

## Why The Short Side Matters

Spot and bonding-curve markets let participants vote yes by buying. Derivatives add the missing no vote. A trader can hedge exposure, short a weak narrative, or express negative information with capital behind it.

That two-sided expression is the reason permissionless perps are more than another product. They complete the market's expressive range. A token economy that can only buy or ignore assets has weaker price discovery than one that can also short and hedge them.

## Publication Boundary

This page supports the historical infrastructure sequence and the "yes/no" market-completeness argument. It does not prove that any current market has safe universal shorting, complete hedge availability, final leverage policy, or permissionless production coverage for every token.
