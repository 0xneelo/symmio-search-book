# Proof Of Value Creation Map

The value-creation side of Neelo's Thiel analysis asks whether Vibe creates real value before asking whether it captures any of it.

The source names several creation surfaces: perp markets for assets that do not yet have mature derivatives, yield on token inventory, utility for projects that do not want to spend scarce stablecoins on market making, capital efficiency versus USDC-heavy vault models, lifecycle continuity from spot to derivatives, listing data, and two-sided price discovery.

The important point is that these are not the same beneficiary. Traders care about exposure, hedging, shorting, and settlement confidence. Projects care about token utility, market feedback, and a path to visibility. LPs or inventory owners care about risk-adjusted yield. Venues and protocols care about better signals for what deserves deeper liquidity.

## How To Read X

The source's X claim should be read as a map of possible value created, not a guarantee that every market creates the same amount. A heavily demanded token with active communities, real hedging demand, and healthy solver coverage creates more X than an inactive token whose market cannot attract flow.

That is why the docs should connect value creation to market evidence:

- Is there real trader demand?
- Is there useful project participation?
- Does LP inventory have a coherent risk/reward profile?
- Does the market produce data that downstream venues can trust?
- Does the market add more than a symbolic listing?

## Publication Boundary

The source includes model-level claims about partner interest, capital efficiency, and perceived demand. Keep exact figures, live partner counts, token deposits, eligibility, and market quality under current source/operator review.
