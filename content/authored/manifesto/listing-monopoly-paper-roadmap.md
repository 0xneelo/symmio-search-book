# Listing Monopoly Paper Roadmap

The Listing Monopoly paper is structured as a sequence: introduce the thesis, define the token lifecycle, map who controls each stage, identify the lifecycle gap, distinguish listing from liquidity and bootstrap, propose permissionless perps as a gap-filling model, then test the strategy through industry implications, conclusion, and Thiel analysis.

That order matters. The reader should not jump directly to "Vibe should list more markets." The paper first explains why listing gates create power, why existing lifecycle stages are fragmented, and why the missing derivatives stage needs scalable liquidity rather than symbol creation alone.

## Reading Order

Use the paper roadmap this way:

- **Introduction:** understand the listing-monopoly thesis and strategic analogy.
- **Framework:** learn the lifecycle stages and where monopoly dynamics appear.
- **Landscape:** see which protocols control which stages.
- **Lifecycle gap:** identify the missing path between DEX graduation and serious perp availability.
- **Listing, liquidity, bootstrap:** separate asset creation from tradeable market creation.
- **Permissionless perps:** study the gap-filling protocol model.
- **Strategic implications and Thiel analysis:** evaluate whether this can become a durable category.

## Publication Boundary

Treat this roadmap as a reading guide, not as a product roadmap or partnership commitment. The source organizes a strategy paper; live launch sequence, integrations, and market-transition rules need implementation review.
