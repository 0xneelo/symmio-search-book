# Proof Of Value Participant Implications

The Proof of Value conclusion translates the thesis into participant implications. That matters because a low-cap perp system is only valuable if its benefits are not confined to one side of the market.

For LPs and token holders, the implication is that held inventory can become productive market inventory. The source's LP claim is not only "earn yield." It is that risk can be better aligned when the party providing token-side liquidity already has exposure to that token.

For traders, the implication is market access. Long-tail tokens often have spot trading before they have serious perpetual access, short expression, hedging, or leverage. Vibe's claim is that solver-mediated execution can help fill that interval while the market is still too young for a mature order book.

For projects, the implication is token utility and market formation. A project can support a perp market without beginning from a large stablecoin commitment or a traditional market-maker mandate. For the ecosystem, the implication is lifecycle completion: more assets can move from issuance to spot trading to derivative validation before they are obvious enough for curated venues.

## The Important Constraint

The implications are reciprocal only if value does not leak out of one constituency into another. LP yield cannot be funded by hiding trader costs. Trader execution cannot be subsidized by unpriced solver risk forever. Project utility cannot rely on permanent deposit optics. Ecosystem value requires markets that produce evidence, not just symbols.

That is why the conclusion belongs next to the validation pages. It names who should benefit, but the living-docs loop has to track whether those benefits materialize.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish final LP yield expectations, trader execution guarantees, project revenue distributions, stablecoin requirements, market eligibility, graduation outcomes, or ecosystem-wide impact claims without fresh primary-source/operator/product/risk/legal/accounting review.
