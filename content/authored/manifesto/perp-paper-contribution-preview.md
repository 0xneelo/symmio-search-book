# The Perp Paper Contribution Preview

The introduction's contribution section is the promise of the paper before the detailed proof. It says the paper will contribute theory, practice, and industry framing.

The theoretical contribution is a categorization framework for perpetual protocols, a formal bootstrap trilemma, and an explanation of why certain architecture combinations fail. This is the part that gives the compendium a precise vocabulary instead of a loose "perps are hard" claim.

The practical contribution is a market-creation architecture, a Z-score maturity metric, and design patterns for graduation. This is where the paper moves from diagnosis to lifecycle mechanics.

The industry contribution is a path toward transparent listing decisions, a way for order-book protocols to identify ready markets, and a vision for completing the token lifecycle. This matters because Vibe is framed as upstream infrastructure for market evidence, not only as a venue competing for already-obvious markets.

## Publication Boundary

Distinguish this preview from the conclusion's contribution map. The introduction tells readers what the paper intends to show; the conclusion summarizes the argument after the framework, trilemma, architecture, technical model, and industry sections have been developed. Live listing partnerships, final Z-score policy, and automatic graduation claims still need product/operator review.
