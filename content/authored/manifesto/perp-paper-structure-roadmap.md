# The Perp Bootstrap Paper Structure Roadmap

The paper structure shows that Neelo's argument is sequential. It does not jump from "perps are important" to "Vibe is the answer." It builds a ladder from market gap, to protocol taxonomy, to existing-architecture analysis, to the bootstrap trilemma, to Vibe's hybrid architecture, to technical mechanisms, industry implications, competitive barriers, and conclusion.

That order is important for the compendium. A reader should not meet the Z-score as an isolated metric. The Z-score belongs after the market-lifecycle problem and after the trilemma, because it measures when a market has earned a different architecture state.

The structure also explains why Volume 02 has many exact pages. Each section answers a different class of question: why the gap exists, what current protocols optimize for, why a static design breaks, how a hybrid design moves over time, what the implementation must measure, and what that changes for exchanges, LPs, traders, and builders.

## Reader Implication

Use this page as the map for readers who ask where to start in the Perp Classes / Z-Score paper. The correct path is not one mega-summary; it is an ordered progression from problem statement to lifecycle architecture.
