# The Game Theory Of Listings

Curated listing systems select on perceived interest, not actual interest, because actual interest cannot be observed until the market exists.

That is the central game-theory problem in Neelo's listing paper. A listing committee or exchange cannot know future demand directly. It must use proxies: social traction, modeled safety, deposits, reputation, projected volume, and narrative strength. Projects then optimize for those proxies because that is what gets them market access.

Permissionless systems preserve more noise, but they also allow the market to discover assets that no curator would have selected in advance. Vibe's strategic claim is that perpetual markets need the same evolutionary openness that DEX spot markets gave token creation and early trading.

## Reader Implication

Readers should understand why "more listings" is not the full point. The deeper goal is to move derivatives from curator prediction to market discovery.
