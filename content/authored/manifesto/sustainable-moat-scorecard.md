# Sustainable Moat Scorecard

Neelo's sustainable-advantages section separates the moat into technical, market-position, and ecosystem layers.

The technical moat is the hybrid architecture, solver expertise, and data accumulation. Hybrid architecture matters because the protocol is designed for traversal between market states. Solver expertise matters because early-market operation is learned under stress. Data accumulation matters because every market can improve the next pricing and graduation decision.

The market-position moat is first-mover experience, integration position, and LP relationships. These are not permanent by default. They become durable only if market creators, LPs, solvers, and downstream venues repeatedly find the system useful.

The ecosystem moat is launchpad connectivity, order-book symbiosis, and the data-provider role. This is the compounding version of the thesis: Vibe becomes harder to displace if other market layers use it as the upstream test environment.

## How To Use The Scorecard

The scorecard should be a verification checklist. For each moat claim, ask what current evidence supports it, what remains source-model-only, and what would falsify it.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish partnership lock-in, permanent advantage, live LP stickiness, or competitor weakness as established facts without current evidence. The source supports the moat categories; durability must be earned and verified.
