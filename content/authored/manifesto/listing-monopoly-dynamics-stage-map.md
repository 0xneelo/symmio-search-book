# Monopoly Dynamics By Lifecycle Stage

Neelo's stage map shows that monopoly dynamics differ across the token lifecycle.

At creation, the source names PumpFun as the dominant early-stage player. The mechanism is graduation control and user experience. This stage is contestable because new launchpads can emerge, but the graduation route still matters because it decides when the token leaves the bonding-curve stage.

At DEX spot, the source uses Uniswap as the historical reference point. The mechanism is liquidity and brand. The category is more contestable than late-stage venues because many DEXs exist, but liquidity concentration and habit still create power.

At late CEX spot and perp stages, contestability falls. Large venues have user bases, liquidity, risk systems, market-maker networks, and order-book depth. New listings become more selective, and long-tail assets face the hardest gate at exactly the moment they need deeper market structure.

## Key Insight

The early lifecycle has become more permissionless. The late lifecycle remains heavily gated. The transition between them is where the opportunity sits: it is inefficient enough to matter and underserved enough for a new layer to define the category.
