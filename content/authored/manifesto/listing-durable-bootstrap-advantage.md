# Durable Advantage In The Bootstrap Layer

Neelo's durable-advantage claim is not that market creation alone is a moat.

If the only capability were adding another market row, competition could commoditize it quickly. The more defensible position is the bootstrap layer: the default place where long-tail perpetual markets become liquid enough to matter and where evidence accumulates about which markets should mature or graduate.

## Why Bootstrap Is The Advantage

The source links this to the earlier lifecycle argument. Order books work best after enough two-sided flow exists. The Section 5 model then describes a path from bootstrap to maturation to graduation.

That makes the monopoly-relevant capability operational, not symbolic. Durable advantage sits in repeatedly turning new markets into tradeable markets, gathering data and user habits during that transition, then using that record to connect markets to deeper venues.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish durable moat, graduation control, data ownership, market-specific liquidity, venue default status, or current competitive advantage as facts without fresh primary-source/operator/product/market/legal review.
