# Listing Data Is A Product

The strongest version of the listing thesis is not that Vibe publishes another leaderboard. It is that early derivative markets produce data that downstream venues currently lack.

Neelo's source names the listing problem directly: venues often rely on spot volume, social metrics, community pressure, competitor listings, and internal judgment. Those signals can be useful, but they are proxies. They can be noisy, gameable, late, or biased toward connected projects.

A Vibe market can produce a different kind of evidence: solver dependency, daily derivative volume, unique trader breadth, position duration, liquidation behavior, funding history, spread quality, and whether flow stays active after the first attention wave. That evidence can inform whether a market is ready for deeper execution.

This turns data itself into a product. Exchanges can use it for listing decisions. Projects can use it to prove market quality. LPs and solvers can use it to price risk. Data providers can package it into demand indicators, quality scores, and research surfaces.

## Publication Boundary

The source includes example threshold rules. Those should not become public product claims until metrics, cadence, formulas, manipulation controls, and destination-venue use are confirmed. The publishable claim is that early derivatives data is valuable; exact listing rules remain review-bound.
