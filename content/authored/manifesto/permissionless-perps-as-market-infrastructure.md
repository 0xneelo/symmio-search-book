# Permissionless Perps As Market Infrastructure

The closing move of Section 7 is that permissionless perps are infrastructure, not only a new trading feature.

For crypto markets, the infrastructure claim is market completeness. If every token can develop a derivative surface, traders get more ways to express long and short views, hedge holdings, discover price, and test demand before a mature venue listing exists.

For DeFi, the infrastructure claim is composability. Perp positions, funding behavior, hedges, and risk metrics can become inputs to lending, yield strategies, risk management, research, and market-quality scoring.

For the industry, the infrastructure claim is fairness and transparency. Market access can move away from opaque gatekeeping and toward observed activity, risk, and maturation data. That does not remove judgment, but it gives judgment a better evidence base.

## Stakeholder Summary

| Stakeholder | Infrastructure effect |
| --- | --- |
| Traders | Access to long, short, leveraged, and hedged exposure earlier |
| LPs / inventory providers | New stage-specific yield and risk-bearing roles |
| Projects | A market-creation path that can prove demand |
| Exchanges | De-risked downstream listing evidence |
| Ecosystem | A more continuous token lifecycle |
| Industry | More transparent, rule-based market development |

## Publication Boundary

This page should be published as a thesis summary, not a claim that every token currently has a Vibe perp, every venue consumes Vibe data, or every DeFi integration exists. Those live status claims require current product and partner evidence.
