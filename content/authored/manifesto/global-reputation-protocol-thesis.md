# The Global Reputation Protocol Thesis

Calling Vibe a global reputation protocol is a category thesis, not a current product guarantee. The source uses the phrase to describe a market system where tokenized claims build reputations through trading history, not through committee endorsement alone.

In this frame, reputation is market history. A claim, token, project, or venue becomes more legible when the market can observe who participates, whether skeptics can pressure it, whether solvers will quote it, whether liquidations and settlements remain clean, whether volume repeats, and whether downstream venues trust the data.

The phrase is strongest when it is tied to those observable mechanisms. A reputation protocol is not a social score. It is a record of costly interactions: intent flow, quote behavior, inventory provision, short pressure, volatility, funding, settlement, and graduation evidence.

The publication guardrail matters. The compendium should not imply that Vibe already dominates global reputation or that every downstream venue uses its data. The claim depends on adoption, integrations, market data quality, solver participation, operator-approved metrics, and production publication choices.

## Reader Implication

Readers should treat "global reputation protocol" as the endgame Vibe is aiming at: a system where market evidence becomes a reputation layer for tokenized information. The current docs should teach the mechanism and mark live dominance claims for future proof.
