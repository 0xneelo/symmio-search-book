# Truth As An Asset Class

Truth as an asset class means that some claims can become market objects whose credibility is tested through capital, liquidity, and adversarial pressure.

The Proof of Value source uses intentionally provocative language: truth stops being only a statement and starts behaving like a market object. The safe reading is not that markets create objective truth. The safe reading is that markets can make belief costly, continuous, and inspectable.

A claim that attracts capital, survives short pressure, keeps liquidity, and matures through volatility has passed a different test from a claim that only circulates as content. That is why the compendium should treat "truth as an asset class" as a thesis about verification infrastructure. The relevant asset is not certainty. It is capitalized belief under pressure.

## Mechanism

Spot markets let believers buy. They do not fully test whether the claim can survive rejection. Vibe's role is to add the derivative layer: leverage, shorting, solver refusal, risk pricing, and market history around claims or assets that would otherwise remain long-only narratives.

That derivative layer matters because it gives skepticism a market action. A participant who believes a claim is overvalued does not have to stay silent or only avoid buying; they can express rejection through short exposure, hedging demand, or refusal to warehouse the risk.

## Guardrail

This thesis needs guardrails. A high market cap can be manipulated. Wealth can overpower quieter evidence. Markets are costly signals, not moral arbiters.

The strongest public version is: truth-like claims become more credible when they survive open, two-sided, capital-at-risk markets. The page should not imply that Vibe, market cap, or price action can certify objective truth.
