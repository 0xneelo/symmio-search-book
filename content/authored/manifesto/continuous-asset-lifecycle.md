# Continuous Asset Lifecycle

Neelo's one-primitive conclusion describes the desired lifecycle: creation, spot trading, permissionless derivatives, and eventual mature venues.

That sequence matters because issuance should not be the end of the story. A token can launch, attract spot attention, enter a derivatives market where the market can express both belief and skepticism, and then mature toward deeper order-book venues if the evidence supports it.

## From Self-Expanding To Self-Correcting

Without the derivatives layer, crypto can keep expanding asset count without necessarily improving market judgment. The source calls for the missing step that makes the stack self-correcting rather than merely self-expanding.

Derivatives are the pressure layer in that lifecycle. They let claims face hedge demand, short pressure, funding, liquidation, and maturity signals. Some assets will look stronger after that process. Others will fail earlier because the negative side can act.
