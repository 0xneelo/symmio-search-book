# Proof Of Value

Proof of Value is Neelo's name for the claim that markets become a verification layer when information becomes cheap to manufacture.

The source argument starts from a convergence: synthetic information is easier to create, synthetic assets are easier to launch, and attention can now become a traded object almost immediately. In that world, authority-based verification weakens because there is too much information for institutions to manually validate. A market with real capital at risk can become a competing filter: people do not merely say they believe a claim, they expose capital to the outcome of being right or wrong.

Vibe's role in this thesis is not just execution. An intent-based, solver-centric system can support niche markets that would be too small or fragmented for a full liquidity pool. A user expresses a directional view; a solver decides whether to quote it; the market can exist with one credible counterparty before it has broad depth.

## Reader Implication

The compendium should treat Proof of Value as a vision layer, not as a current dashboard feature. It explains why permissionless derivatives matter beyond leverage: they can turn demand, skepticism, and conviction into a measurable market signal.
