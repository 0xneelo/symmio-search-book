# Vibe As A Listing Oracle

Neelo's industry section uses "listing oracle" in the market-information sense, not as a price-feed claim. The problem is that downstream venues have to decide which derivative markets deserve operational support before they can observe how those derivative markets behave.

The current process leans on proxies: spot volume, social metrics, community pressure, competitor listings, relationships, and internal judgment. Those signals can be directionally useful, but they do not directly answer the question a perp venue needs answered: will this market produce durable two-sided demand, manageable risk, and usable liquidity?

Vibe's role in this source is to create the missing evidence layer. A live early perp market can produce Z-score behavior, derivative volume, unique trader breadth, position duration, liquidation behavior, and funding history. Those are market facts generated by trading, not only by narrative.

## From Vibes To Rules

The source's strongest contrast is between vibes-based listing and rule-based graduation. Instead of asking a committee to guess whether a market will work, the downstream venue can inspect whether the market has already shown activity, balance, and risk behavior.

That does not mean every threshold in the source is production policy. It means the compendium should teach the shape of the decision: a market should earn deeper venue support because it has generated evidence.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish final Z-score thresholds, required volumes, trader-count windows, venue commitments, or automated listing rules from this page alone. The source supports Vibe as an evidence-producing listing oracle; live formulas and partner behavior remain product, risk, legal, and operator review.
