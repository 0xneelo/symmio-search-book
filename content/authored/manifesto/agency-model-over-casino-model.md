# Agency Model Over Casino Model

Neelo's Information/Trade source contrasts two market-creation models. The first is the pool-first casino model: launch a token, seed an AMM or bonding curve, and let attention plus passive liquidity define the initial game. The second is the agency model: a user expresses an intent, a solver evaluates the risk, and the trade happens only if a counterparty can price it.

The agency model matters because it changes what has to exist before a market can begin. A bonding curve needs committed inventory and a pool design before anyone knows whether the claim deserves a market. An intent-based OTC model can begin with a specific request and a specific counterparty. The market starts as priced judgment rather than as an always-on pool.

That is why the source treats Vibe as a better architecture for long-tail information and tokenized credibility. The system is not promising that every claim becomes true or that every thin market deserves execution. It is saying the first market interaction can be mediated by a solver instead of by a blind curve.

This page should remain careful with the phrase "truth market." The source uses that language, but publication prose should translate it into verifiable mechanics: intents, solver pricing, counterparty acceptance, risk controls, and settlement.

## Reader Implication

The agency model explains why Vibe is not trying to make every early token look like a mature AMM market. It tries to let market access begin with an intent and a priceable counterparty, then mature only if demand and risk data justify it.
