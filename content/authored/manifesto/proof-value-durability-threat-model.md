# Proof Of Value Durability Threat Model

The durability section asks whether value survives after the first wave of attention.

Neelo's source names several durability factors: structural capital efficiency, risk alignment, a defense hierarchy, lifecycle progression from bootstrap to maturity, partnership strategy, and early project commitment. Those are useful, but the compendium should treat them as a threat model rather than a finished conclusion.

Durability fails if a better architecture emerges, if LP yield is not sufficient for the risk, if volume stays below expectations, if competitors can copy the model faster than data compounds, or if market quality does not improve with maturity.

## What A Durable Proof Would Show

The public docs should eventually turn the source's scorecard into evidence:

- markets continue trading after launch attention fades;
- solver residual risk is bounded and priced;
- LP returns come from market activity, not deposit optics;
- market data improves listing and graduation decisions;
- mature markets become less dependent on bootstrap defense;
- partnerships produce usable distribution or venue continuity.

Until those checks are source-backed, the scorecard is a strategy thesis. That is still valuable. It tells readers what must be proven and gives the living-docs loop a measurement agenda.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish capital-efficiency magnitude, partner counts, switching costs, first-mover advantage, competitor timelines, or the final scorecard as audited current fact without fresh primary-source/operator/product/risk/legal/market review.
