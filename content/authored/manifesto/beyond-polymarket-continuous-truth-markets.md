# Beyond Polymarket: Continuous Truth Markets

Prediction markets are a useful starting point for thinking about markets as verification infrastructure, but Neelo's Information/Trade source argues that they do not scale to the long tail of synthetic reality.

The limitation is not that binary markets are useless. It is that they require setup, a bounded event, and a resolution path. That makes them appropriate for elections, sports outcomes, rates decisions, and other public questions with a definable endpoint. It makes them weak for the world the source is worried about: endless AI-generated claims, tokenized narratives, memes, micro-projects, and local facts that will never receive a formal market or a clean judge.

The alternative model is continuous. Instead of asking "will this event happen?" and waiting for resolution, a tokenized claim trades continuously. Market cap, liquidity, and price persistence become the running signal. This is not objective truth in a courtroom sense. It is market-tested credibility: a claim that can keep attracting capital has a different status from one that collapses when challenged.

Vibe matters in this frame because spot buying is only half of that verification loop. If holders can buy but skeptics cannot efficiently short, the market mostly measures enthusiasm. A continuous truth market needs the negative side so false or overhyped claims can be pressured by capital instead of merely ignored.

## Reader Implication

Readers should not reduce Vibe's thesis to "prediction markets, but perps." The deeper claim is that permissionless derivatives can create a continuous verification layer for assets and claims that are too numerous, too early, or too weird for resolved prediction markets.
