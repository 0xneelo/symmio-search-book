# Proof Of Value Source Map

Neelo's Proof of Value source is not one page of product copy. It is a source corpus: information theory, market verification, short-side expression, intent architecture, solver economics, listing power, and the final "Thumbs Down" metaphor all live in one argument. This page keeps that corpus navigable without asking the public docs to expose every extracted subsection as final prose.

## Reading Order

Start with `authored-proof-of-value` for the thesis: markets can become a verification layer when synthetic information is cheap. Then read `authored-proof-authority-to-proof-value` for the transition away from authority-based truth, and `authored-information-validation-crisis` for the broader verification problem.

The source's "beyond Polymarket" sections belong with `authored-beyond-polymarket-continuous-truth-markets`. They explain why resolved prediction markets are not enough for long-tail claims, tokenized narratives, and markets that need continuous pressure rather than a single event outcome.

The "missing NO button" and "Thumbs Down" sections route to `authored-truth-markets-no-button`, `authored-financialized-rejection-thumbs-down`, and `authored-thumbs-down-mechanism-stack`. Those pages translate the source's cultural metaphor into a mechanism: capital should be able to reject a claim, not only endorse it.

The intent and solver sections route to `authored-intents-and-solvers`, `authored-vibe-intent-architecture`, `authored-hybrid-solver-liquidity-waterfall`, and `authored-protocol-owned-solver-public-option`. The source describes solvers as active verification actors because they quote, refuse, hedge, and price risk. That is a source-model claim about the architecture's role; live solver behavior still needs implementation and operator review.

The listing-power sections route to `authored-game-theory-of-listings`, `authored-strait-gate-market-filter`, and `authored-listing-monopoly`. They connect Proof of Value to market access: if listings are controlled by centralized gates, early demand is hard to prove; if Vibe can surface tradable evidence earlier, venues and communities get a stronger signal.

## Publication Boundary

Do not treat every example in the Proof of Value source as a current Vibe product claim. AI-to-AI solver language, 70 percent LP pass-through language, long-tail fact-checking metaphors, and visual/product hooks are useful source-model material. They are not final production guarantees without current primary-source, product, risk, legal, accounting, and operator review.

The publication-safe claim is narrower: the Proof of Value corpus explains why permissionless derivatives matter as a verification layer, why short-side expression changes information markets, and why an intent-and-solver system is the proposed mechanism for making long-tail claims economically testable.
