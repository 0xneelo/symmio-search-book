# Perp Bootstrap Source Navigation Map

Neelo's Perp Classes / Z-Score overview, conclusion, and Thiel-analysis fragments are source-navigation material for the bootstrap thesis. They should orient the reader toward the exact authored pages, not become thirteen separate public pages.

The overview fragments describe the paper's job: explain why permissionless perpetual market creation is hard, classify existing perp protocols, formalize the bootstrap trilemma, and show why Vibe's hybrid architecture is designed to move a market from bootstrap to maturity. Route readers who need the ordered paper map to `authored-perp-paper-structure-roadmap`, and route readers who need the short thesis to `authored-perp-paper-abstract` or `authored-bootstrap-trilemma`.

The key-theme and reading-order fragments are useful as audience routing. Executives should start with the abstract, market gap, and conclusion. Technical readers should follow the framework, landscape, trilemma, architecture, and technical deep dive. Competitive readers should use the framework, landscape, and competitive-analysis pages. Builders should use the architecture, solver, oracle, settlement, funding, and position-lifecycle pages.

The conclusion fragments close the argument: market creation remains primitive, opaque, and manually gated; a hybrid lifecycle architecture can create early markets, measure maturity, and graduate markets when evidence supports it. Route contribution questions to `authored-perp-paper-contribution-map`, industry questions to `authored-market-lifecycle-industry-implications`, and vision questions to `authored-permissionless-perps-vision-restated`.

The TODO, acknowledgments, references, and appendices fragments are boundary signals. They name missing work: HIP-3 data, fee comparisons, lifecycle-gap sizing, smart-contract architecture, hedging algorithms, threat model, Z-score thresholds, roadmap milestones, author acknowledgments, comprehensive references, formal proofs, protocol comparison data, and solver specifications. Do not publish those placeholders as complete claims. Use them as a checklist for product, research, risk, security, and operator review.

The Thiel-analysis fragments add the strategy frame. They apply value creation versus value capture to Vibe: creating new perp markets for long-tail tokens is the X, while fees, solver spread, LP management, data products, and graduation relationships are possible Y surfaces. Route monopoly questions to `authored-thiel-monopoly-verdict-boundary`, value-capture questions to `authored-value-capture-durability-scorecard`, and technology-moat questions to `authored-thiel-proprietary-technology-test`.
