# Lifecycle Gates As Market Power

The listing-monopoly argument starts with a simple observation: a token becomes economically relevant only when it can move through market gates. Launch, graduation, spot liquidity, centralized exchange access, perp access, and institutional eligibility are not just milestones. They are control points.

Neelo's lifecycle framework makes those control points visible. PumpFun controls the early creation and graduation moment. DEXs control permissionless spot access. Binance, Hyperliquid, and other mature venues control much of late-stage perp availability. Each layer owns a different kind of gate, and each gate changes what the token can become.

The power comes from dependency. Communities follow their token to whichever venue creates the next useful market surface. Projects optimize for the gate they need next. Traders and LPs route to the place where liquidity is real enough to act. That turns listings into user acquisition, fee capture, data capture, and ecosystem influence.

The compendium should therefore avoid treating "listing" as an admin action. A lifecycle gate is a market-formation event. It changes who can trade, who can hedge, who can short, who can provide liquidity, and who can collect the evidence that the market deserves its next stage.

## Reader Implication

When readers ask why Vibe cares about permissionless derivatives, the answer is not only "more assets." The answer is lifecycle control. A protocol that owns the missing gate between early spot markets and mature perp venues can become the default bridge between two disconnected parts of crypto.
