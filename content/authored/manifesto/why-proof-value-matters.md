# Why Proof Of Value Matters

Proof of Value matters because each participant has a different failure mode.

LPs need to know whether the return compensates the actual risk they carry. Traders need execution quality, liquidity, and confidence that profitable positions can settle. Projects need a market path whose benefits justify inventory, attention, and operational cost. The ecosystem needs capital-efficient price discovery and hedging, not a structure that extracts value through hidden risk transfers.

The source's contribution is to make those questions explicit. A permissionless perp market can look active while still being fragile if one participant class is subsidizing another without knowing it.

## The Four Audience Test

The compendium should answer Proof of Value questions by audience:

- LP or inventory owner: what risk do I bear, and why is the yield coherent?
- Trader: who backs my fill, and what happens if the market moves hard?
- Project: what utility does my inventory create, and what do I receive?
- Ecosystem or venue: does this market produce better validation and listing evidence?

If the docs cannot answer those questions, the product may be interesting but not yet self-serve for serious market participants.

## Publication Boundary

Audience-specific value claims must stay source-labeled. Do not publish exact LP terms, project economics, trader guarantees, or ecosystem impact as current facts without approved product/accounting/risk/legal sources.
