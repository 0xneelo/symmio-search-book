# Long-Tail Verification Needs Throughput

The long-tail problem is a throughput problem. A verification system that depends on manual market creation, formal question design, or explicit resolution cannot keep up with a world where new claims and new tokens can be generated continuously.

Neelo's source uses AI-generated media and instantly tokenized claims to make the point. The issue is not only misinformation. It is volume. The market cannot wait for a committee, resolver, or mainstream platform to decide which claims deserve markets. Most claims will be too small, too local, too fast-moving, or too strange for that process.

This is where permissionless derivatives become more than speculation. If a market can form around any sufficiently priceable token or claim, then verification can happen at the edge. Participants express belief or skepticism through positions. Solvers decide whether the risk is quoteable. Liquidity, spread, funding, short interest, and persistence become evidence.

That does not make every long-tail market correct. It makes more claims testable. In a world of synthetic abundance, increasing the number of claims that can face capital is a different kind of information infrastructure.

## Reader Implication

Readers should understand the long tail as a scale argument. Vibe's thesis depends on making more claims priceable, not on pretending every claim deserves a mature order book on day one.
