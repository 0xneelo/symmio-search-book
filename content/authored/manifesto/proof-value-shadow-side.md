# The Shadow Side Of Proof Of Value

Proof of Value is powerful because it attaches cost to belief. It is dangerous if the docs make that sound like perfect truth.

Neelo's crisis-of-verification source names the shadow side directly: market-cap-as-truth can drift toward a plutocracy of truth, where the wealthy or the coordinated crowd can push a narrative harder than quieter participants can resist. The source does not present markets as morally pure. It presents them as a costly filter in a world where lying and synthetic content can be nearly free.

That distinction matters for the Vibe x Symmio compendium. A market can reveal commitment, liquidity, skepticism, and stress. It cannot guarantee objective truth by itself. The docs should frame Proof of Value as a verification layer with adversaries, not as a substitute for judgment, source review, risk controls, or governance.

## Reader Implication

Readers should see Vibe's thesis as a harder information market, not an infallible one. Market price is evidence. Short interest is evidence. Solver refusal is evidence. None of those are absolute truth; they are economically costly signals that can be inspected and challenged.
