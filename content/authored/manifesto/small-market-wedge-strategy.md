# The Small-Market Wedge Strategy

The small-market wedge is the strategic answer to a common category mistake: Vibe is not trying to win all perpetual volume on day one. The source argues that the first category is narrower: tokens with spot attention and no serious perp market.

That market is small enough to define and painful enough to matter. Incumbent order books focus on assets with proven demand. Vault protocols struggle to scale long-tail risk efficiently. The wedge is the lifecycle gap where traders want exposure before conventional venues can justify the market.

From there, the expansion path is concentric. Start with the long-tail markets nobody else can serve well. Accumulate risk data, solver experience, LP behavior, and listing evidence. Expand across chains, asset classes, integrations, data products, and mature venue handoffs.

This is a strategy page, not a monopoly guarantee. The source's Thiel framing is useful because it forces precise market definition and durability analysis. The docs should avoid unsupported claims that Vibe cannot be competed with. The safer and stronger claim is that solving bootstrap-to-maturity creates a category incumbents are not structurally built around.

## Reader Implication

Readers should stop comparing Vibe only to mature perp venues. The initial question is whether Vibe can dominate the underserved market for early derivative creation. If it can, the later integrations and data products become more plausible.
