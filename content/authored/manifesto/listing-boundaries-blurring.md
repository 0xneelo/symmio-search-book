# How Listing Controller Boundaries Blur

Neelo closes the landscape section by warning that lifecycle stages are not sealed boxes.

Controllers expand. A creation platform can add swap or DEX-like functionality. A late-stage exchange can build a launchpad. A perp venue can add spot markets. The strongest platforms do not stay politely inside their original category when adjacent stages carry user flow and fee capture.

That expansion blurs the edges of the lifecycle map. It creates competition at the boundaries: launchpads move toward DEXs, exchanges move toward launch, and perp venues move toward spot.

## Why The Gap Persists

The source's key point is that boundary-blurring does not erase the structural gap.

Each controller still tends to dominate its core stage. PumpFun-style systems are strongest at creation and graduation. Uniswap-style systems are strongest at permissionless spot. Binance and Hyperliquid-style systems are strongest once markets are late enough to justify deeper infrastructure.

The missing stage remains the long interval where a token has spot activity and demand, but not mature perp availability. In Neelo's frame, that is the space roughly between small post-graduation markets and established large-cap assets.

## Publication Boundary

Treat examples of platform expansion as source-time examples. Current launchpad, DEX, spot, perp, market-cap, and platform-boundary claims need fresh primary-source review before publication.
