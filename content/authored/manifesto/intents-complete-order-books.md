# Intents Complete Order Books

The strongest version of the order-book argument is true: mature markets want deep, competitive, synchronous liquidity. The Vibe thesis does not require pretending otherwise.

The gap appears before maturity. Long-tail assets, newly issued tokens, and fragmented attention markets often do not begin with both sides ready to quote at scale. An order book can be the best end state while still being a weak bootstrap mechanism. Neelo's "Ode to OrderBooks" frames Vibe as the missing layer beneath the mature venue: Vibe discovers whether demand exists, gives solvers a way to price that demand, and creates a path for markets to graduate into deeper execution layers.

This is why "intent-based derivatives" is the better category name than "another DEX." An intent says what a trader wants before the system knows who will satisfy it. A solver can evaluate the request, quote it, hedge it, or decline it. That workflow lets markets form around demand instead of requiring a complete book before discovery begins.

## Reader Implication

Order books are not the enemy. Empty books are. The compendium should show Vibe and Symmio as infrastructure that handles the pre-book and solver-mediated parts of the derivatives lifecycle, then links back to order books where order books are strongest.
