# Proof Of Value Thesis Restated

The restated thesis is the compact version of the Proof of Value argument: Vibe's hybrid architecture is meant to create measurable, sustainable economic value for LPs, traders, and projects by combining capital efficiency, risk alignment, solver-managed residual exposure, and early-market lifecycle progression.

That sentence should be read as a thesis, not as a launch audit. The value of the conclusion is that it shows what must be proven. If Vibe is right, low-cap perp markets do not have to choose between expensive generic stablecoin backstops and reflexive token-margined designs. They can use token inventory where token holders already bear the asset risk, solver-funded stablecoin operations where stablecoin exposure is needed, and dynamic risk controls where the market is too young for a passive venue.

## How To Use The Restatement

The compendium should use this page as the final short-form answer to "what is the Proof of Value thesis?" Then it should route readers to the evidence pages:

- the four-constituency framework for who receives value;
- LP value and token-holder inventory alignment;
- trader and project value;
- economic clarity and risk allocation;
- comparative advantage against USDC-vault and token-margined alternatives;
- validation and sustainability criteria.

The thesis becomes credible only when those pages remain source-backed and review-bound where production facts are not yet approved.

## Publication Boundary

Do not convert the restated thesis into a blanket product guarantee. Capital-efficiency magnitude, partner traction, live LP yield, revenue distribution, payout behavior, solver hedge quality, defense-stack policy, regulatory posture, and security claims need current primary-source/operator review before final publication.
