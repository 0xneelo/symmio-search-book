# Prediction Markets Still Need A Resolution Layer

Prediction markets are powerful, but Neelo's Information/Trade source treats them as only the first step toward market-based verification.

The constraint is resolution. A prediction market usually needs a question to be created, bounded, traded, and later resolved by a human, oracle mechanism, or formal adjudication process. That works best for large public events with known endpoints. It works less well for the long tail of claims that are local, weird, AI-generated, niche, or too numerous for anyone to curate.

This is why the source distinguishes prediction markets from continuous market verification. A prediction market asks whether an event happened. A continuous token or derivatives market keeps asking whether capital still believes, whether liquidity remains, whether skeptics can attack the claim, and whether the claim survives over time.

The compendium should keep this distinction precise. Vibe is not "Polymarket with perps." The deeper thesis is that permissionless derivatives can make verification continuous for assets and claims that never receive a clean prediction-market resolution path.

## Reader Implication

If a reader asks why prediction markets are not enough, route them here first. The answer is not that prediction markets fail; it is that they still need market creation and resolution machinery that cannot cover infinite synthetic information.
