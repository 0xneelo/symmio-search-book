# Listing Monopoly Conclusion: Summary Of Contributions

The conclusion restates the Listing Monopoly paper as a contribution to crypto market-power analysis.

The central contribution is the listing-monopoly thesis: control over the token lifecycle, especially listing gates at each stage, is a primary source of market power. The source maps the British Empire analogy into crypto: control the routes where value flows, and control the industry around those routes.

## What The Paper Adds

The paper adds a token-lifecycle framework. It follows a token from creation, through DEX spot access, into perpetual markets and late-stage venues. Different protocols dominate different stages, and the conclusion argues that power concentrates at the gates between those stages.

It also adds a landscape map. Binance, PumpFun, Uniswap, and Hyperliquid are not treated as interchangeable venues. They are examples of different lifecycle controllers, each with a different monopoly mechanism.

The conclusion then sharpens the lifecycle-gap claim. The gap between DEX graduation and perp availability disconnects fair-launch ecosystems from institutional infrastructure. Section 4Z adds an important correction: the real power is not listing language alone, but tradeable markets with generalized liquidity generation.

## Missing Layer

The final contribution is the hypothetical missing layer. Permissionless perpetuals are framed as the bridge between early spot markets and mature derivative venues. The source's contribution is not a live product guarantee. It is a market-structure claim: a protocol that fills the missing derivatives interval could complete the permissionless progression from transfer to swap to creation to derivatives.

The conclusion includes source-time market-share examples and market-cap thresholds. Treat them as source-context until refreshed. Do not publish current market-share, graduation, listing-threshold, liquidity, or dominance claims without fresh primary-source/operator/product/risk/legal review.
