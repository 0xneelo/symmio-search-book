# The Listing Monopoly

The listing monopoly thesis says that power in crypto concentrates wherever a venue controls which assets become tradable, at which stage, and under which market structure.

Neelo's framework maps the token lifecycle as a sequence of gates: creation, DEX trading, centralized exchange access, and perpetual market availability. Each gate has different operators and incentives. The important claim is that derivatives access is one of the least permissionless parts of that lifecycle. Many tokens can be created and traded spot, but only a small subset ever receive serious perp markets.

This creates a structural gap between early-stage token creation and mature market infrastructure. A token can graduate from a launchpad and trade on DEX venues, yet still lack systematic derivatives, hedging, and short-side price discovery until it becomes large enough for incumbent venues to care.

## Reader Implication

The compendium should frame Vibe as an attempt to compete for lifecycle control by filling the derivatives gap, not simply as a UI for more listings. The strategic question is who owns the path from token birth to mature market access.
