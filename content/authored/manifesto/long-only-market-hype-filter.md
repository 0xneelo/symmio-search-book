# Long-Only Markets Filter For Hype

The missing-NO-button source starts with the long-only flaw. If a market lets users buy or ignore but not short, price mostly measures positive attention. Skepticism has no equally direct expression.

That does not make every long-only price meaningless. It means the signal is incomplete. Buyers can push a narrative upward, but informed skeptics can only abstain, post, or wait. The market receives the capital of believers while undercounting the capital and conviction of people who know why the claim is wrong.

For Vibe's thesis, this is the reason permissionless perps matter beyond "more leverage." A market that can only say yes can confuse momentum with validation. A market that lets capital say no can reveal whether the narrative survives adversarial pressure.

Publication docs should avoid saying price is never useful in long-only markets. The safer claim is that long-only markets are asymmetric filters: they overrepresent enthusiasm and underrepresent informed rejection.

## Reader Implication

When readers ask why Vibe needs a short side, start here. The problem is not that buying is bad. The problem is that a market without a clean negative side cannot fully test what it is pricing.
