# Profitable Fact-Checking Loop

The source makes a sharp incentive claim: fact-checking becomes more scalable when it can be profitable.

Traditional fact-checking often depends on reputation, duty, moderation, or public service. Those can matter, but they do not always move as fast as synthetic content and tokenized attention. A market can add another incentive: if a participant finds a false claim early and prices it correctly, they can earn from the correction.

That is the profitable fact-checking loop. Detect weak evidence. Express the negative view through a short. Let the market test the claim. If the claim breaks, the skeptic earns because they improved the price signal.

The docs should also state the boundary. Profit motive does not guarantee truth. Short sellers can be wrong, manipulative, or overlevered. The value of the loop is that it introduces an adversary to hype, not that it makes every adversary right.

## Reader Implication

Vibe's short-side thesis is strongest when it is treated as an incentive system. The market pays participants who find and price fragile claims, while risk controls limit how destructive that process can become.
