# Uniswap As Permissionless Swapping Infrastructure

Neelo uses Uniswap as the lifecycle case study for permissionless spot exchange.

After Ethereum made contracts permissionless, Uniswap made swapping permissionless. A token no longer needed a centralized listing committee to become tradeable on a DEX. Any ERC-20 could have a pool, and liquidity providers could supply the market without a traditional market-maker mandate.

That historical achievement matters for the Listing Monopoly thesis because it proves that permissionless listing at one lifecycle stage can reach monopoly-scale importance. Uniswap did not remove every market problem. It removed the spot-listing gate for a broad class of tokens.

## Lifecycle Position

In the lifecycle map, Uniswap sits after token creation and graduation, but before late CEX listings and perpetual markets.

That position makes it a middle-early stage controller. It can connect newly launched tokens to broader spot trading, but it does not itself solve the derivatives gap. A token can have a Uniswap pool, visible spot activity, and real speculative demand while still lacking leverage, shorting, hedging, and mature two-sided derivative price discovery.
