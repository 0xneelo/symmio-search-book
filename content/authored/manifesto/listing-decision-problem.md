# The Listing Decision Problem

Neelo's introduction separates market creation into two problems: architecture and information. Even if a venue can technically list a market, it still has to decide whether the market deserves scarce attention, liquidity, risk, and operational support.

Today that decision is usually made from proxies. The source names spot volume, social metrics, community pressure, internal judgment, and business relationships. Each proxy can help, but each can also be noisy, gameable, biased, subjective, or conflicted.

That is the sense in which listing can become "vibes-based." The issue is not that every listing team is careless. The issue is that before a derivatives market exists, venues lack objective derivatives evidence. They are guessing future market quality from signals adjacent to the market.

Vibe's strategic role is to turn that guess into evidence. If early perp intent, solver pricing, position behavior, liquidation behavior, funding pressure, and market maturity can be observed before a mature order-book listing, then downstream venues can make listing decisions from market history rather than proxy theater.

## Publication Boundary

Do not claim any specific exchange uses Vibe data, any Z-score threshold is final, or any listing process is fully automated from this section alone. The source supports the problem statement and the direction of travel: early derivatives evidence can make listing decisions more objective.
