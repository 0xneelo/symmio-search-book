# The Project Alternative Cost Stack

Vibe's project-side value proposition should be documented as an alternative-cost problem, not only as a promise of a new market.

The source names the usual choices available to projects and token treasuries. A centralized exchange listing can require applications, compliance review, fees, stablecoin commitments, and relationships. A professional market maker can require retainers, spread agreements, inventory policy, and stablecoin allocations. Single-sided spot liquidity can create sell-pressure and impermanent-loss tradeoffs. Doing nothing preserves inventory, but it also leaves the token without a derivative market, hedging path, or short-side signal.

That stack is important because it explains why token inventory can be attractive. Many projects have the asset they issued or manage. They may not have abundant stablecoins, and they may not want to spend scarce treasury liquidity before the market has proven demand. If Vibe lets a project support a perp market with the asset it already holds, the cost profile changes.

## What Vibe Is Trying To Remove

The project does not get a free market. It still accepts custody, market, inventory, disclosure, and reputational risk. The source-backed inversion is narrower: the project may be able to avoid leading with a stablecoin deposit, a separate market-maker hiring cycle, or a centralized listing dependency before derivative demand exists.

That inversion is strategically useful for the compendium. When a project asks "why would we use Vibe?", the answer should not begin with a generic list of features. It should compare the project alternatives:

- spend stablecoins to pursue venue access;
- pay or negotiate with a market maker;
- support spot liquidity and accept its tradeoffs;
- leave inventory idle;
- use token inventory to help create a measured perp market.

The last option is Vibe's wedge. It turns project inventory into market-formation capital and makes the resulting flow observable.
