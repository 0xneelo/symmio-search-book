# Listing Monopoly Thesis Statement

The Listing Monopoly thesis is that listings are choke points in the token lifecycle. Every token must pass through market gates before it becomes economically relevant: creation, spot access, liquidity, derivatives, and mature venue treatment.

Protocols that dominate one of those gates can capture disproportionate value because users, projects, liquidity, data, and attention follow the gate. The lifecycle remains fragmented: one system may dominate launch, another may dominate spot swapping, and another may dominate late-stage perpetuals.

Neelo's key extension is that permissionless perpetuals are the unfinished part of the permissionless market stack. Transfer, programmable value, swapping, and creation became open first. Derivatives remain comparatively gated. The protocol that fills that stage for the long tail can become the bridge between early token activity and mature capital markets.

## Thesis In One Sentence

Control the gate that lets long-tail tokens become credible derivatives markets, and you influence the token lifecycle rather than merely participating in one venue.

## Publication Boundary

This page should be used as the source-backed thesis statement. Do not publish it as proof that any live Vibe market already controls the lifecycle, captures monopoly economics, or has completed the category. Those are execution claims requiring market evidence.
