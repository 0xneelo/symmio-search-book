# Strategic Implications Summary

Neelo's Section 6 summary is that strategic implications cut across the whole lifecycle, but the dominant theme is partnership over competition.

A gap-filling perp protocol would affect protocol designers, launchpads, DEXs, order-book protocols, centralized exchanges, traders, LPs, and the broader ecosystem. The reason is simple: the protocol would not just add one more venue. It would change what happens between early spot markets and mature derivative markets.

## The Non-Zero-Sum Path

The source's strategic path is not a direct war against every surrounding player. Launchpads and DEXs create upstream flow. Order books and CEXs provide downstream maturity. Traders, hedgers, and LPs supply the activity that tests whether a market deserves to keep moving through the lifecycle.

The gap-filling layer's role is to make that middle leg legible. It starts with low-cap perps, integrates with the existing lifecycle, and creates evidence that adjacent venues can use.

## Durable Value Capture

The summary connects strategy to value capture. Durable value does not have to come from trapping every market forever. It can come from owning the bridge where early derivative demand first becomes measurable.

That is why the Section 6 close matters for the compendium. It turns the listing-monopoly thesis from an adversarial slogan into a lifecycle strategy: start in the neglected middle, complement existing gates, and capture value by making markets more continuous.
