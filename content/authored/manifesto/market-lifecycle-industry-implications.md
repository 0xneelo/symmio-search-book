# Market Lifecycle Implications For The Industry

Neelo's industry-implications section turns the architecture thesis into a stakeholder map.

For protocol designers, the lesson is to design for evolution. A protocol choice is not only a point solution; it decides which market states the system can survive. Measuring maturity matters because automation without the right signal is only another hardcoded policy.

For exchanges, the implication is complementarity. A bootstrap layer can feed mature venues with better evidence: demand, flow quality, risk behavior, and readiness signals. The useful frame is not "Vibe versus every venue." It is upstream market creation feeding downstream execution layers.

For traders and LPs, the implication is stage-specific risk. Bootstrap markets can create new opportunities, but they should not be explained with the same risk language as mature markets. A reader needs to know whether they are funding market creation, trading a maturing market, or using a venue-ready market.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish partner status, integration status, institutional-grade claims, live Z-score automation, or final exchange behavior from this section alone.
