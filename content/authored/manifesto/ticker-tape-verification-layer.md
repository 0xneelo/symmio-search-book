# The Ticker Tape Verification Layer

Neelo's source argues that the internet's verification layer changes when information becomes cheap to manufacture and assets become cheap to launch.

The old mental model is a library: information is stored, searched, cited, and judged by institutions, media brands, experts, or communities. That model still matters, but it weakens when synthetic content can be produced faster than institutions can review it.

The new mental model is closer to a ticker tape. Claims become attached to markets. Those markets produce running signals: price, liquidity, volatility, persistence, short pressure, solver appetite, and funding stress. None of those signals is perfect truth. Together they create a costly, adversarial verification surface that unsupported content lacks.

This page should keep the rhetoric bounded. The source says truth becomes an asset class; production docs should translate that into mechanics. A tokenized claim with a market is not automatically true. It is exposed to capitalized belief and capitalized rejection. That exposure is the verification primitive.

## Reader Implication

The answer engine should use this page when readers ask why Vibe treats trading data as information. The durable claim is that markets can add costly evidence to claims, not that prices are infallible truth.
