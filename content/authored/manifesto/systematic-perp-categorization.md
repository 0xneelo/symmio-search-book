# Why Perp Protocols Need Systematic Categorization

Neelo's framework starts by rejecting brand-level comparison. A perpetual protocol cannot be understood only by asking whether it is a CLOB, AMM, vault, solver system, or app-chain. The useful question is what architecture choice it makes for matching, collateralization, and insurance.

The source says the landscape lacks a common vocabulary. Without one, protocols can all claim innovation while readers lose the ability to compare them objectively, identify their limits, predict which use cases they fit, or understand why a design fails under bootstrap pressure.

The categorization is therefore an editorial tool and a risk tool. Matching asks how trades meet. Collateralization asks who pays winners. Insurance topology asks whether a market's failure is contained or shared. Those questions are more durable than protocol marketing copy.

## Reader Implication

Route here when a reader asks why the compendium spends so much time on taxonomy. The answer is that taxonomy prevents false equivalence. It lets the docs compare systems by architecture and market state rather than by slogan.
