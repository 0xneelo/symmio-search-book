# How Permissionless Perps Relate To Existing Venues

Neelo frames the hypothetical gap-filling perp protocol as complementary to existing listing controllers.

The point is not to beat every venue at its own stage. PumpFun-style systems create and graduate early tokens. DEXs provide spot discovery and permissionless liquidity. Hyperliquid-style order-book venues can receive markets once demand is mature enough. Binance remains focused on established large markets.

The missing stage is between those systems. A permissionless perp layer would serve assets that already have spot attention but do not yet justify major venue infrastructure.

## Why Complementarity Matters

Complementarity changes the strategic posture. The protocol does not need to replace launchpads, DEXs, order books, or CEXs. It needs to make the lifecycle continuous enough that each adjacent venue becomes more valuable.

Upstream venues create token flow. The gap-filling layer turns that flow into derivative evidence. Downstream venues can then evaluate markets with better demand history, short-interest evidence, volume quality, and risk behavior.

## Publication Boundary

This page supports a relationship model, not a confirmed integration map. Do not publish current launchpad, DEX, Hyperliquid, Binance, CEX, or downstream venue partnerships without fresh primary-source/operator/product/legal review.
