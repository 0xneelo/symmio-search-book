# Listing Controller Comparative Summary

Neelo's comparative table turns the landscape section into a lifecycle map.

Each protocol is not merely a competitor. Each controls a different stage, uses a different listing mechanism, and creates power through a different moat.

PumpFun controls creation and graduation through rule-based launch infrastructure and early user flow. Uniswap controls DEX spot through permissionless pools, liquidity, brand, and historical network effects. Hyperliquid controls late perp infrastructure through performance, order-book depth, and HIP-3-style listing access. Binance controls late spot and perps through selective listing, user base, liquidity, and brand. Tier 2 and Tier 3 CEXs occupy narrower regional or niche mid-spot roles.

## What The Comparison Shows

The table's conclusion is structural: crypto listing control is fragmented by lifecycle stage.

The early pole is permissive. The late pole is selective. The middle remains under-served, especially for tokens that are too mature to be only launchpad assets but too early to justify deep order-book or centralized exchange treatment.

That is the opening for the Listing Monopoly thesis. A protocol that can serve the missing middle does not need to beat every incumbent at its strongest stage. It needs to own the lifecycle interval the others structurally skip.
