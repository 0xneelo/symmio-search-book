# Proof Of Value Requires Validation And Sustainability

Proof of Value is not a slogan. It has to survive contact with data.

Neelo's validation section separates early commitment from long-term proof. Early partner interest can show that projects understand the value proposition, but sustainable proof requires operating evidence: trading volume, retention, LP yield, solver performance, stress behavior, and whether markets mature toward more natural netting over time.

That is the right standard for the compendium. The docs should not stop at "markets can launch." They should show what would prove that a market is becoming healthier.

## Sustainability Criteria

The source's sustainability path is lifecycle-based. In bootstrap, the solver is more important because natural two-sided flow may not exist. In maturity, more flow can net trader-to-trader, spreads can tighten, solver residual risk can decline, and the market can become a candidate for deeper venue integration.

The source also distinguishes real trading revenue from deposit-driven optics. A sustainable model should not depend on new LP deposits to pay existing participants. It should earn from real market activity, manage stress through a disclosed defense stack, and expose risks rather than burying them in promotional claims.

## What The Docs Should Track

The living-docs loop should turn this into a measurement agenda: which markets show real flow, which markets remain solver-dependent, which ones stress insurance or ADL thresholds, and which graduate toward better execution. That is how a manifesto becomes accountable.
