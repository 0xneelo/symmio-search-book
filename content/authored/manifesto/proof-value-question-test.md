# The Proof Of Value Question

The Proof of Value question is whether a permissionless perp design creates value that is real, measurable, aligned, and sustainable.

That is a stricter test than "can the protocol list a market?" A listed market can still fail if LPs demand impossible compensation, traders cannot trust execution or settlement, projects pay more than they receive, or risks are pushed into hidden corners of the system.

Neelo's introduction frames Proof of Value as a four-part test:

- value is created for each participant class;
- participants benefit together rather than at one another's expense;
- the market can operate under realistic stress;
- early evidence supports the claims without being mistaken for final proof.

## Why This Is The Entry Point

This page should route readers who ask why the compendium treats economics as seriously as architecture. For long-tail perp markets, value is not proven by technical possibility alone. It is proven when the market can name who participates, what they receive, what they risk, and what evidence would show the system can keep operating.

The rest of the Proof of Value framework then decomposes that question into LP value, trader value, project value, ecosystem value, economic clarity, comparative advantage, validation, and durability.
