# The End Of Narrative-Based Listings

Narrative-based listing is what happens when a venue has to infer future demand before a market exists. The source frames the listing problem around two signals: actual interest and perceived interest. Permissioned venues usually list from the second because the first cannot yet be observed.

That substitution explains why listings become political. A venue evaluates spot volume, community pressure, narrative strength, relationships, auctions, and prior exchange listings. Those signals can be directionally useful, but they are proxies. They do not answer the question the venue really cares about: whether traders will use the perp market enough for it to function.

Vibe's vision is to produce the missing signal directly. Let the market exist early in a constrained environment. Let traders express demand. Let solvers quote, hedge, and learn the market. Let the resulting history become evidence for whether the asset deserves deeper execution.

This is why "vibes-based listings" is not just a meme about bad curation. It is a symptom of missing infrastructure. When early derivatives cannot exist, every later venue has to guess. When early derivatives can exist, listing decisions can be pulled toward observed market behavior.

## Reader Implication

The compendium should teach market creators that Vibe changes the listing argument. Instead of asking for belief, a project can point to actual perp demand. Instead of competing on narrative alone, the market can compete on revealed trading behavior.
