# Listing Monopoly Conclusion: Key Insights

Neelo's conclusion compresses the Listing Monopoly paper into four insights.

First, listings create monopoly power. A protocol that controls a lifecycle gate can capture users, fees, data, network effects, and strategic influence. The conclusion adds the Section 4Z correction: in derivatives, listing and liquidity compound. A visible market without generalized bootstrap liquidity can still be empty.

## The Gap Is Structural

Second, the DEX-to-perp gap is structural rather than accidental. Order books struggle to bootstrap thin markets. Manual listing is expensive. No incumbent naturally owns the missing stage. Those architectural, economic, and incentive constraints explain why the gap persists.

That makes the gap a design problem, not just a business-development opportunity.

## Partnership Beats Competition

Third, the strongest gap-filling strategy is complementary. Launchpads, DEXs, order books, and CEXs already occupy natural lifecycle territories. A bridge layer becomes valuable by connecting those territories rather than pretending every adjacent venue must be replaced.

## Permissionless Derivatives Are The Frontier

Fourth, the source frames derivatives as the next permissionless frontier. Crypto made transfer, swaps, and token creation progressively more open. The remaining question is whether derivatives can become similarly permissionless without losing risk discipline.
