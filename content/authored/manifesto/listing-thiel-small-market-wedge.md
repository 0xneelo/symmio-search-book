# Listing Monopoly Small-Market Wedge

The Listing Monopoly Thiel source defines the initial market narrowly.

The target is not every perpetual market. It is the underserved segment of tokens that have enough spot-market life to matter but not enough maturity to earn major perp listings. That wedge is strategically useful because it is bounded, painful, and not well served by incumbent venues.

## Concentric Expansion

The source describes expansion as concentric rather than immediate maximalism. Start with gap tokens. Then expand across more chains, more token classes, mid-cap markets that want better derivatives access, order-book integrations, and eventually the infrastructure layer for perpetual market creation.

That sequencing is important for the docs. A category-defining protocol does not need to begin by replacing Binance, Hyperliquid, Uniswap, or launchpads. It begins by serving the part of the lifecycle those venues leave open.

## Publication Boundary

Market-cap bands in the source are source-time strategy examples, not current product eligibility. Do not publish exact supported ranges, launch requirements, chain coverage, target assets, dominance claims, or expansion timelines without fresh primary-source/operator/product/risk review.
