# Partnership Over Venue War

The listing-monopoly thesis can sound adversarial, but Neelo's strategic section is more useful than a simple venue war. A gap-filling perp layer is complementary to launchpads, DEXs, order-book protocols, and late-stage exchanges because it serves a lifecycle stage they do not naturally own.

Launchpads and DEXs benefit if graduated tokens can receive derivative access earlier. That keeps communities inside the decentralized ecosystem longer and gives projects a clearer path from spot attention to two-sided market expression. Order-book protocols benefit if upstream markets generate real demand data before a deeper listing. Centralized exchanges benefit from better listing intelligence and a cleaner pipeline of proven markets.

The strongest Vibe story is therefore not "replace every downstream venue." It is "create the upstream evidence those venues wish they had." A market that proves volume, short interest, liquidity quality, and solver economics is easier for a mature venue to evaluate than a market competing only on narrative.

This posture also clarifies the product boundary. Vibe does not need every market to remain in its bootstrap state forever. Owning the bridge can be more valuable than trapping markets inside one execution model.

## Reader Implication

Readers should understand Vibe as a lifecycle connector. For CLOB ecosystems, it can be an upstream discovery and graduation layer. For launchpads, it can extend the value of graduation. For traders and LPs, it defines a different risk regime before mature liquidity arrives.
