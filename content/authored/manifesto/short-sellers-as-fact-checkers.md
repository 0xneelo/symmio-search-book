# Short Sellers As Fact-Checkers

The Proof of Value source recasts short selling as an information function. In a long-only market, skepticism is weak. A user can refuse to buy, complain, or leave, but the false or overhyped claim may continue to float on attention.

Shorting changes the incentive. If a claim is inflated, wrong, or fragile, a skeptic can risk capital against it. If the skeptic is right, the market pays them for revealing the weakness. The source calls this the missing negative feedback loop: a market needs a way to say no.

That does not mean short sellers are always right or virtuous. It means they create an economically motivated adversary. They make overstatement more expensive and make bad claims easier to attack before they become entrenched.

## Why Vibe Matters

Most long-tail tokens are hard to short at launch. That is the gap. Vibe's thesis is that intent-based, solver-mediated perps can bring the short side earlier, without requiring every claim to have a mature order book or deep AMM pool first.

The docs should frame shorting as market discipline, not as negativity for its own sake. A two-sided market is healthier because it lets evidence, belief, and rejection all carry cost.
