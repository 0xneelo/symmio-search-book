# Listing Monopoly Future Directions

Neelo's future-directions section turns the Listing Monopoly thesis into a research agenda.

The first agenda item is empirical validation. The gap should be quantified: market-cap ranges, time to perp availability, number of affected tokens, PumpFun graduation rates, post-graduation paths, Binance listing timing, post-listing performance, and whether a gap-filling perp layer changes lifecycle continuity.

## Theory Work

The second agenda item is theoretical extension. Listing monopoly dynamics can be modeled game-theoretically. Vertical integration can be studied across combinations such as launchpad plus DEX, or exchange plus launchpad. Partnership structures for gap-filling protocols also need a more formal analysis.

That matters because the strategic claim should not stay purely narrative. If lifecycle control is a moat, the compendium should be able to explain the incentives and failure modes that make it durable or fragile.

## Regulatory And Policy Work

The third agenda item is regulatory and policy analysis. The source asks how permissionless perps interact with securities regulation, how CEX and DEX competitive dynamics change, and what policy choices would support lifecycle completion.

The docs should not answer those legal questions casually. The useful contribution is to mark them as future work rather than pretending a market-structure thesis settles regulation.

## Publication Boundary

This page is a research agenda. Do not publish legal conclusions, policy recommendations, current empirical measurements, market-performance claims, or partner structures without fresh primary-source/operator/legal/product review.
