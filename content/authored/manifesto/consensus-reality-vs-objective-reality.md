# Consensus Reality Versus Objective Reality

Neelo's Beyond Polymarket section draws a sharp distinction between external resolution and continuous market judgment.

A prediction market usually depends on a resolver. The event ends, someone decides what happened, and the market settles. That gives the system a clean endpoint, but it also limits the kinds of questions it can handle. Most tokenized claims do not have a neat final oracle. Projects evolve. Narratives decay. Communities change. Liquidity appears and disappears. Reality is often continuous rather than resolved.

The source calls the alternative the Market's Eye View. In publication prose, the safer phrase is sustainable consensus: a token, claim, or narrative is not proved true because it pumped once. It becomes more credible if it can sustain price, liquidity, participation, and adversarial pressure over time.

## What This Does Not Mean

This is not a denial that objective facts exist. It is a claim about market architecture. Markets often cannot access a perfect external view on demand. They can only expose claims to capital, skepticism, liquidity, time, and risk. That process may reveal weak claims, but it can also lag, overreact, or be manipulated.

For Vibe, the useful conclusion is that continuous markets can act as ongoing verification surfaces where prediction markets cannot easily define a one-time resolution event.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish "truth is whatever the market says" as a final claim. The source supports a market-process thesis: sustainable consensus is useful evidence when objective resolution is unavailable, but it remains fallible and needs risk controls, liquidity context, and source-aware interpretation.
