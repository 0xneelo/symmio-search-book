# How Listings Create Monopoly Power

Neelo's listing-monopoly source breaks listing power into four mechanisms: user acquisition, network effects, gatekeeping, and fee extraction.

User acquisition starts with communities. A token community brings holders, attention, capital, and public promotion. When a platform lists that token, it does not only add a market; it gives the community a reason to route attention and activity toward the platform.

Network effects compound the listing. More listed assets attract more users. More users make the venue more attractive for future assets. Once a platform becomes the default place for a lifecycle stage, both users and projects face higher switching costs.

Gatekeeping is the strategic layer. A listing decision, or a decision to remove listing barriers, can influence which tokens get serious market access. That power can appear as curation, auctions, or permissionless mechanisms; the common element is control over the route into liquidity.

Fee extraction is the economic result. Trading fees, listing fees, and spread capture flow to the platform that facilitates the market. The listing layer becomes a place where attention, flow, data, and economic capture meet.

## Reader Implication

The compendium should treat listings as market infrastructure, not clerical approvals. A listing creates a route for a community to become tradeable, hedgeable, shortable, measurable, and economically legible.

## Publication Boundary

Use this page for the mechanism of listing power. Do not use it as a live claim that any current Vibe venue already has dominant user acquisition, irreversible network effects, gatekeeper status, or fee capture at scale without fresh market evidence.
