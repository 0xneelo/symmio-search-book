# Referral Sustainable Fee Flow Objective

The referral conclusion gives the operating objective: optimize for listings and sustainable fee flow while preserving trust under adversarial conditions.

That means a referral system is not successful merely because it brings more accounts into the funnel. It is successful when those accounts help create markets that can attract demand, generate fees, and remain credible after abuse controls and accounting review.

## What Sustainable Fee Flow Requires

Sustainable fee flow needs both sides of the market-formation loop:

- market supply: high-quality listings, active markets, repeat listers, and time-to-first-listing;
- market demand: traders per active market, fee-generating volume, turnover, and concentration checks.

The metrics source supports that separation. Supply without demand is not enough. Demand without clean attribution can still be gamed. Fee flow without integrity controls can become a payout dispute.

## Publication Boundary

This page should not publish a live fee-share formula, referral payout cadence, private partner split, or TGE weighting rule. Those remain under public-economics and operator review. The source-backed claim is the objective: referral growth should be judged by quality-adjusted listings and sustainable fee flow, not raw invite volume.

## Reader Implication

When users or partners ask whether a referral campaign is working, the answer should route to market-creation and fee-flow evidence. Code issuance and signups are supporting indicators, not the north star.
