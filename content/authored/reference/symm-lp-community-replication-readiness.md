# SYMM LP Community Replication Readiness

The SYMM LP source does not say every token community can copy the case study immediately. It names readiness conditions that should exist before a community tries to replicate the model.

The first condition is trader demand. The market needs an active base willing to express views through perps. Without recurring trader participation, the LP may have inventory but no useful market to support.

The second condition is narrative and participation quality. A token community needs enough credible market attention that recurring positions, funding transfer, and turnover can be observed. Pure announcement interest is not enough.

The third condition is liquidation integrity. The market needs liquidity and risk controls that let the system close, hedge, or manage positions under stress. Otherwise the case can look attractive in calm conditions and fail when profitable traders, gaps, or thin liquidity appear.

The fourth condition is transparent LP reporting. Depositors need to understand realized versus marked PnL, drawdown, attribution, utilization, and benchmark performance before capital expands.

## Reader Implication

Replication readiness is a filter. It keeps the SYMM case from becoming a generic pitch to every community and turns it into a checklist: demand, narrative, liquidation quality, and reporting discipline must exist before scaling.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish a token community as "replication-ready" until product, risk, accounting, and market-operations owners confirm the criteria and evidence. The source-backed claim is the readiness checklist, not a live eligibility approval.
