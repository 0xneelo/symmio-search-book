# Vibe Trading Notion Perpetual Bid Boundary

The Vibe Trading Notion workspace adds one important product narrative to the public source map: a perpetual listing can be framed as a recurring bid system for a project token, not only as a source of short pressure.

The fetched workspace page and its child pitch pages describe a market-creation loop. A project supplies token-side inventory, the solver manages quotes and risk, and trading activity can produce spreads, funding, liquidation proceeds, or net trader-loss inflows. In the source's framing, those inflows can be routed into buybacks or ongoing token support. The pitch is meant to answer a project's fear that perps necessarily create spot dumps or liquidation cascades.

The Notion source also separates early and mature market states. Early markets may require explicit bootstrapping and can carry opportunity cost for treasury inventory. More balanced markets can net long and short exposure more efficiently, leaving the LP or treasury with less directional exposure while flow still produces fees. The useful public idea is the transition path: bootstrapped liquidity can mature into a market whose trading activity funds support rather than only consuming support.

Projects should read this page as the safest public summary of the Notion workspace: Vibe can pitch a perpetual market as a liquidity-and-buyback engine when solver risk, token inventory, and market flow are managed correctly.
