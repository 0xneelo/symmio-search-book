# SYMM LP Executive Interpretation Boundary

Neelo's executive-summary interpretation is explicit: the results are concrete point-in-time results from one case and one regime. They show strong potential for treasury or holder collateral deployment on Vibe, but they are not a guaranteed steady-state yield.

That boundary is central to the compendium. The case is useful because it demonstrates a real mechanism: token inventory can function as market backstop capacity, and directional crowding can create LP-favorable economics. It is not enough to prove that every token community should deploy large LP inventory or expect the same result.

## Low Volume Does Not Mean No Signal

The source calls out a second important interpretation: the outcome was achieved in a low-volume environment. The core driver was directional crowding, not high churn from frequent opens and closes.

That matters because an LP case can work for reasons other than fee volume. Funding, trader PnL transfer, liquidation-related flows, and marked exposure can dominate in a thin market when one side is crowded and wrong.

## Reader Implication

When a reader asks "what should I conclude?", route here before scaling pages. The safe conclusion is proof of potential under an observed regime. The unsafe conclusion is a permanent yield promise.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish future LP yield, general treasury advice, vault suitability, or scaling capacity from this interpretation without operator, accounting, legal, risk, and implementation review. The source supports a potential-and-boundary claim, not a guarantee.
