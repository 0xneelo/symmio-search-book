# Vibe Project Audit And Exit Rights

The project-listing terms include two practical control surfaces: audit access and exit mechanics.

The audit path gives the client a way to request access to trading records and PnL statements with prior written notice. The source says this uses a mutually agreed third-party auditor under confidentiality obligations, with audit costs borne by the client.

## Exit And Delisting

The same terms include termination rights, community notice and wind-down language around delisting, final reporting, and dispute-resolution language. Those terms matter because a project listing is not only a launch event. It also needs a controlled path if the service ends, the market is delisted, or the relationship changes.

## Reader Guidance

For projects, the answer is not "trust the dashboard." The public terms describe a formal audit path and an exit/wind-down framework. For traders, the existence of delisting language is a reminder that a listed market can have service-level and safety boundaries; a listing does not guarantee permanent support or unchanged terms.

This page is a documentation summary, not legal advice. The applicable agreement or statement of work controls exact rights, notice, reporting, and dispute mechanics.
