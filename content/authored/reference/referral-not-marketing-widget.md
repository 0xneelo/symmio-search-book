# Referral Is Not A Marketing Widget

Neelo's referral conclusion says the program is not a standalone marketing widget. It is a structural layer connecting user acquisition, market creation, and fee distribution.

That sentence is the architecture boundary. A generic referral program optimizes for signups, links, and codes. Vibe's referral layer has to connect those actions to tradable market supply, demand quality, and auditable fee flow.

## Why The Distinction Matters

If referrals are treated as marketing only, the docs can over-emphasize invites, code counts, and short-term campaigns. Those numbers are easy to inflate and easy to misunderstand.

If referrals are treated as market-formation infrastructure, every public answer has to ask a harder question: did this referral motion produce better markets, cleaner attribution, sustainable fee distribution, and a reward ledger that can survive review?

That is why the referral pages keep returning to identity, attribution, reward accounting, anti-gaming, rollout discipline, and KPI governance. Those are not back-office details. They are the difference between a growth loop and a liability.

## Reader Implication

When readers ask "what is the point of referrals?", the answer should not stop at "invite friends and earn rewards." The deeper answer is: referrals are one way Vibe turns distribution into market creation while keeping fee flow and rewards accountable.

## Publication Boundary

This page can publish the category distinction between marketing referrals and market-formation infrastructure. It should not imply that every invite, code activation, reward pack, private partner agreement, or future points settlement is live, transferable, or economically final. Live payout formulas, private overlays, reward-accounting details, anti-gaming enforcement rules, and launch sequencing remain governed by their specific policy and implementation pages.
