# Symmio Settlement Costs And Affiliates

Symmio's cost model is product-specific. The official settlement-costs page says settlement costs are not one-size-fits-all and points traders and builders to separate product categories such as perps and pair trading. The current trading-fees page likewise redirects fee detail toward product-specific settlement-cost pages and notes that rates can be set by symbol and affiliate.

The source also states a key builder-side asymmetry: solvers and market makers do not pay settlement fees. That policy is framed as a way to encourage professional market makers and solvers to provide tighter, more efficient markets.

## Frontend Builder Economics

Symmio's settlement-costs page says a portion of each platform fee is allocated to the exchange's affiliate partner, meaning a frontend builder integrated with Symmio can earn a share of fees generated by traders executing through that interface.

That is relevant to Vibe x Symmio docs because Vibe is a product surface on top of a protocol layer. Readers need to distinguish protocol-level settlement-cost categories from product-specific fee presentation and any Vibe-specific revenue disclosure.

## Publication Boundary

Do not publish fee percentages here unless the product-specific settlement-cost page and Vibe's public disclosure boundary both support them. The safe claim is structure: product-specific settlement costs, zero settlement fees for solvers and market makers, and affiliate/frontend-builder fee-credit semantics.
