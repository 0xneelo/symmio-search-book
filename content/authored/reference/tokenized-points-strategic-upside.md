# Tokenized Points Strategic Upside

The tokenized-points-perps source is hypothetical, but it explains why reward accounting has strategic consequences.

If point-linked exposure ever becomes a transferable on-chain object, the design space expands beyond internal rewards. Value can circulate before a canonical token event, risk can move from passive holders to willing counterparties, builders can compose around the object, and pricing feedback can appear before TGE.

## The Five Upsides

The source names five possible upsides:

- liquidity before token launch;
- risk transfer for participants who do not want passive exposure;
- builder composability without centralized coordination;
- faster market feedback through earlier pricing surfaces;
- new growth loops where referral and pack demand can compound once exposure is tradable.

Those are design-space benefits, not a product announcement. They only become credible if the underlying point, pack, artifact, claim, transfer, and eligibility states are precise enough for outside builders to rely on.

## Why The Docs Need This Page

Readers may ask why a referral or rewards page spends so much time on accounting boundaries. The answer is that composability increases consequence. A sloppy off-chain points display is bad UX. A sloppy value-bearing on-chain object can become bad market infrastructure.

## Publication Boundary

This page explains strategic optionality in a hypothetical source model. It does not announce tokenized points, fractional wrappers, spot markets, perp listings, liquidity programs, market-maker support, transferability, or any user-facing financial product.
