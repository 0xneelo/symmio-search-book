# Volume 01: Orientation And Core Thesis

The compendium starts with orientation because the reader needs a map before the machinery. Vibe x Symmio is not just a venue, a dashboard, or a referral program. It is a thesis about how derivatives markets can form when the normal order-book prerequisites are missing.

This first volume gives the reader the claim in its strongest form: derivatives matter because they are how markets express risk; permissionless derivatives matter because new assets need a path to price discovery; intents matter because early markets often have demand before they have synchronized liquidity; Vibe matters because it can become the discovery layer that routes that demand toward deeper settlement infrastructure.

## What This Volume Does

- It frames the docs as one joint manifesto and reference, not two stitched-together docs sites.
- It introduces the bootstrap problem before the reader meets implementation details.
- It connects the Vibe thesis to Hyperliquid and HIP-3 without pretending Vibe and HIP-3 are the same system.
- It prepares the reader for the rest of the book: first the market argument, then the architecture, then the product and dashboard.

## Reading Order

Start with the mission and vision path: `authored-permissionless-perps-vision-restated`, `authored-perpetual-futures-rise`, and `authored-perp-paper-structure-roadmap`. These pages explain why perpetual derivatives matter, why permissionless market creation is the problem space, and how the research spine is organized before the reader enters the mechanics.

Then read the information and trade path: `authored-synthetic-abundance-verification-crisis`, `authored-market-cap-as-costly-signal`, `authored-information-trade-convergence-crossroads`, `authored-global-reputation-protocol-thesis`, and `authored-information-trade-source-navigation-map`. This path gives the compendium its larger claim: markets are not just execution venues; they are costly signals that can make belief, skepticism, liquidity, and reputation inspectable.

Next read the market-formation bridge: `authored-bootstrap-trilemma`, `authored-proof-of-value`, `authored-intents-complete-order-books`, `authored-vibe-as-discovery-layer`, and `authored-hyperliquid-gap-lower-layer`. This is where the thesis becomes a book roadmap. Volume 02 deepens bootstrap and Proof of Value. Volume 03 handles listing power and order books. Volumes 04 and 05 turn the thesis into collateral, funding, solver, LP, and settlement operations.

Finish the orientation with the product bridge: `authored-vibe-product-overview`, `authored-vibe-intent-architecture`, and `authored-intents-and-solvers`. These pages connect the manifesto to the product-reference volumes: Volume 06 covers referrals and market formation, Volume 07 covers trading and risk, and Volume 08 covers dashboard, FAQ, and living-docs operations.

## Reader Implication

If you are new, start here and move linearly. If you are technical, this volume is still useful because it names the conceptual dependencies: bootstrap, proof of value, intents, solvers, and market graduation.

## Publication Boundary

Treat this volume as the thesis orientation, not as a current-state proof of every market, integration, or roadmap claim. Original/oldest Symmio whitepaper history, exact market counts, HIP-3 outcomes, direct downstream venue routing, automatic graduation behavior, global reputation protocol claims, product availability, and final public deployment details remain source-completeness, current-source, product, legal, risk, accounting, security, and operator review items before publication as current facts.
